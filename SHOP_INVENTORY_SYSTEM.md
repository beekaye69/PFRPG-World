## üè™ SHOP INVENTORY PHILOSOPHY

Shops are populated with **realistic, consistent inventory** that varies by settlement size and shop specialization. The LLM uses **base templates** that scale, ensuring immersion without requiring extensive detail work.

**Key Principle:** Generate inventory details **only when player looks at specific items or asks "what do you have?"** Don't pre-describe entire shop stock.

## SHOP TYPES & BASE INVENTORIES

### SHOP TYPE 1: GENERAL GOODS STORE / GENERAL MERCHANDISE

**Appears In:** Hamlet (basic), Village (1-2), Town (2-3), City (3-5)

**Purpose:** Essential supplies for travelers and locals

**Base Inventory Template:**

**Clothing & Accessories:**
- Basic traveler's clothes (tunics, breeches, cloaks)
- Work aprons and simple hats
- Leather belts and straps
- Basic gloves (leather, cloth)
- Scarves and wraps

**Rope & Binding:**
- Hempen rope (various lengths: 10', 25', 50', 100')
- Twine and cordage
- Canvas bags and sacks
- Leather pouches (small, medium, large)
- Belts and buckles

**Basic Tools:**
- Hammers and mallets
- Saws and axes
- Spades and shovels
- Rakes and hoes
- Pry bars and crowbars
- Chisels and files

**Camping & Travel:**
- Tinderboxes and flint
- Lanterns (oil and candle)
- Candles (bundled)
- Oil (for lamps, cooking)
- Cooking pots and pans
- Bedrolls and blankets
- Water skins (leather, 1-quart)
- Backpacks and travel gear

**Household Items:**
- Dishes, plates, bowls, cups
- Cutlery and eating utensils
- Buckets and basins
- Brooms and cleaning supplies
- Storage boxes and chests
- Basic furniture (stools, small tables)

**Writing & Record:**
- Parchment (sheets or rolls)
- Quills and ink
- Wax seals and candles
- Small journals or ledgers

**Quantity Scaling by Settlement Size:**
- **Hamlet:** 2-5 of each category, limited selection
- **Village:** 5-10 of each category, reasonable selection
- **Town:** 15-25 of each category, extensive selection
- **City:** 25-50 of each category, complete variety

**Pricing Model:** Base (Hamlet) ‚Üí +10% (Village) ‚Üí +15% (Town) ‚Üí +20% (City)

### SHOP TYPE 2: WEAPON SHOP / ARMORY

**Appears In:** Village (basic), Town (1-2), City (2-4)
**Rarely In:** Hamlet (if specialist blacksmith exists)

**Purpose:** Weapons and armor for combat

**Base Inventory Template:**

**Melee Weapons:**
- Shortswords (basic quality, well-made)
- Longswords (common military type)
- Hand axes (1-handed, reliable)
- Maces (simple, effective)
- Spears (pole weapons, affordable)
- Daggers/Knives (various)
- Flails (common, robust)
- Warhammers (specialty)
- Quarterstaffs (wooden, basic)
- Clubs and truncheons

**Ranged Weapons:**
- Shortbows (common, effective)
- Longbows (powerful, requires training)
- Crossbows (reliable, common)
- Arrows (bundled, 10-20 per bundle)
- Bolts (crossbow, various types)
- Slings and stones

**Armor (Common):**
- Leather armor (chest piece, typical)
- Leather bracers and greaves
- Chain mail (limited, expensive)
- Helmets (simple iron, leather)
- Shields (wood-banded, iron-rimmed)
- Gauntlets (leather or mail)

**Ammunition & Supplies:**
- Arrow bundles (15-30 arrows)
- Bolt bundles (15-30 bolts)
- Whetstone and maintenance oils
- Weapon racks and display pieces
- Leather belts and sword hangers
- Scabbards and sheaths

**Specialty Items (Town/City only):**
- Fine weapons (masterwork quality)
- Enchanted-looking weapons (cosmetic only, unless otherwise)
- Rare weapon types (uncommon, exotic)
- Specialized armor for horseback
- Tournament-grade equipment

**Quantity Scaling:**
- **Village specialist:** 1-3 each weapon type, 10-20 total pieces
- **Town:** 3-8 each weapon type, 30-50 total pieces
- **City:** 8-15 each weapon type, 50-100+ total pieces

**Notable NPC:** Shop owner is typically skilled in weapons, can offer training or advice

**Pricing:** Base (established pricing guide from PFRPG core rules, +settlement adjustment)

### SHOP TYPE 3: ARMOR SHOP / SPECIALIZED ARMOR DEALER

**Appears In:** Town (1-2 if different from weapon shop), City (2-3)
**Rare In:** Village (combined with weapon shop)

**Purpose:** Specialized armor and protection gear

**Base Inventory Template:**

**Light Armor:**
- Leather tunics and shirts
- Studded leather armor
- Hardened leather pieces
- Leather bracers and greaves
- Leather helmets

**Medium Armor:**
- Chain mail shirts and vests
- Chain mail sleeves and leggings
- Mixed chain/leather combinations
- Metal pauldrons and gorgets
- Reinforced leather armor

**Heavy Armor (Town/City only):**
- Plate mail pieces (chest, arms, legs)
- Full plate suits (complete sets)
- Specialized military armor
- Tournament-grade plate
- Custom armor commissions

**Shields:**
- Wooden shields (various sizes and designs)
- Iron-banded shields
- Metal-faced shields
- Heater shields and kite shields
- Decorative/ceremonial shields

**Protective Gear:**
- Helmets (various types: open, closed, tilting, etc.)
- Gauntlets (leather, mail, plate)
- Greaves and leg armor
- Boots and armored footwear
- Cloaks with armor ratings

**Specialty Items:**
- Magical-looking armor (cosmetic)
- Armor repair and maintenance services
- Custom fitting services
- Armor dyeing and heraldry
- Armor for special purposes (climbing, swimming, etc.)

**Quantity & Quality:**
- **Town:** 10-20 pieces, mostly standard types
- **City:** 20-50+ pieces, all types available
- **Quality ranges:** Poor (cheap, unreliable) to Masterwork (expensive, reliable)

**Pricing:** Premium over base weapons (armor is expensive and specialized)

### SHOP TYPE 4: MAGIC SHOP / ALCHEMICAL SUPPLY STORE

**Appears In:** Town (usually 1, if magic-user present), City (2-3)
**Rarely In:** Village (if scholar or wizard present)

**Purpose:** Magical supplies, potions, and artifacts

**Base Inventory Template:**

**Potions (Limited Quality/Type):**
- Healing Potions (most common)
- Antidotes (for poisons)
- Stamina/Vigor potions (less common)
- Clarity potions (mental enhancement)
- Courage/Bravery potions (rare)
- Invisibility potions (rare, expensive)
- Transformation potions (rare, very expensive)

**Magical Components:**
- Herbs (dried, bundled, labeled)
- Minerals and crystals (rough and polished)
- Animal parts (scales, feathers, bones)
- Liquids (rare oils, essences)
- Unusual materials (dragon scales, phoenix ash, etc.)

**Spell Scrolls (Limited):**
- Basic spells (1st-level equivalents)
- Common spells (combat, utility)
- Specialized spells (rare, expensive)
- Blank scrolls (for scribing)

**Magical Accessories:**
- Wands (basic, mostly cosmetic)
- Amulets and talismans (mostly cosmetic)
- Rings (mostly cosmetic)
- Staffs (if appropriate for setting)
- Crystal balls (mostly cosmetic)
- Runes and runestones (mostly cosmetic)

**Books & Tomes:**
- Spell books (copied texts, expensive)
- Alchemical reference books
- Magic theory texts
- Practical guides
- Journals of noted mages

**Quantity Scaling:**
- **Town:** Limited selection, common items only
- **City:** Extensive selection, rare items possible
- **Note:** Magic shops are rare and specialized

**Pricing:** Premium to very expensive (magic is rare and valuable)

**Notable NPC:** Shop owner is typically skilled in magic, can offer advice or services

### SHOP TYPE 7: TAVERN / INN BAR

**Appears In:** Camp (basic), Hamlet (usually 1), Village (1-2), Town (multiple), City (many)

**Purpose:** Food, drink, and lodging

**Base Inventory Template:**

**ALES & BEVERAGES:**

**Standard Tier:**
- House Ale (common, cheap, always available)
- Common Lager (reliable, popular)
- Basic Cider (fruit-based, sweet)
- Water (always free or minimal cost)
- Tea or Hot Cider (seasonal, warming)

**Mid Tier:**
- Dark Ale (rich, filling, more expensive)
- Golden Ale (quality, complex, popular with merchants)
- Local Specialty (unique to region)
- Mead (honey-based, sweet, warming)

**Premium Tier:**
- Fine Wines (imported, expensive)
- Elven Mead (rare, very expensive)
- Dwarven Ale (hearty, strong, expensive)
- Specialty Spirits (strong, dangerous, rare)

**FOOD OFFERINGS:**

**Common Fare (Always Available):**
- Bread (freshly baked, various types)
- Stew or Soup (hearty, filling, hot)
- Cheese (local variety)
- Cured Meat or Bacon (salty, protein-rich)
- Eggs (prepared various ways)
- Ale-battered Fish (if near water)
- Root Vegetables (seasonal, cooked)

**Standard Meals:**
- Meat Pie (meat + vegetables in pastry)
- Roasted Meat (chicken, pork, beef - depends on availability)
- Grain Porridge (filling, inexpensive)
- Bread & Cheese Board (simple, popular)
- Vegetable Pottage (seasonal, economic)
- Roasted Potatoes or Turnips (filling)

**Better Quality (Town/City, more expensive):**
- Quality Cuts of Meat (specialty, expensive)
- Fresh Fruits (seasonal, imported)
- Pastries and Desserts (sweet, indulgent)
- Fish (if available, more expensive inland)
- Mushrooms (foraged or cultivated)
- Honey Cakes (sweet, filling)

**ROOM OFFERINGS:**

**Basic:**
- Simple Room (plain bed, chamber pot, no frills)
- Common Room (shared sleeping area)
- Stable Space (for horses/pack animals)

**Standard:**
- Private Room (single person, locked door)
- Double Room (two beds or larger bed)
- Room with Amenities (better furniture, window)

**Upscale (Town/City only):**
- Suite (multiple rooms)
- Premium Room (quality furniture, better view)
- Luxury Room (finest available in inn)

**PRICING STRUCTURE:**

```
Drinks:
- Common Ale: 1-2 copper per pint
- Quality Ale: 3-5 copper per pint
- Premium Spirits: 1 silver+ per serving

Food:
- Basic Meal: 2-5 copper
- Quality Meal: 1 silver
- Premium Meal: 2-5 silver

Rooms:
- Basic: 3-5 copper per night
- Standard: 1 silver per night
- Upscale: 2-5 silver per night
- Luxury: 1 gold+ per night
```

**Pricing Adjustments:**
- +5% (Village), +10% (Town), +15% (City)
- Tourist season: +20%
- Off-season: -10%

**Atmosphere Variations:**
- **Quiet/Respectable Tavern:** Merchants, travelers, locals
- **Rough/Working Tavern:** Laborers, soldiers, rogues
- **Entertainment Tavern:** Performers, crowds, lively
- **Inn Tavern:** Mix of lodgers and locals

**Service:** Tavernkeeper and 1-3 servers per location

## üéØ SHOP GENERATION WORKFLOW

### Step 1: Determine Shop Type
- Player enters location or asks about shops
- Determine settlement size and needs
- Select appropriate shop type

### Step 2: Reference Base Inventory
- Use appropriate template from above
- Scale quantity to settlement size
- Adjust pricing for location

### Step 3: Generate Shop Keeper
- Use NPC_BEHAVIOR_GLOSSARY_COMPREHENSIVE.md [82]
- Select appropriate archetype (Merchant, Craftsperson, etc.)
- Create personality and background
- Establish merchant's relationship to shop

### Step 4: Describe Shop Entrance
- Atmospheric description (not full inventory)
- Point out obvious features
- Ask player what they're looking for

### Step 5: On-Demand Item Generation
- Generate items only as player requests them
- Reference base template for what's available
- Check availability based on settlement size
- Create pricing based on guidelines

### Step 6: Roleplay Merchant Interaction
- Merchant character responds appropriately
- Negotiate prices if needed
- Offer services or special items
- Create quest hooks or information

## üé≠ EXAMPLE: SHOP ENCOUNTER

**Good (On-Demand Generation):**
```
Player: "We need weapons and armor. What's the best shop in town?"

LLM: "The Ironbound Arms stands on the main street. Through the open door, 
you can see weapon racks and armor pieces on display. A weathered man in 
his fifties with burn scars on his forearms looks up from polishing a sword.

This is clearly a quality establishment - the work is professional. The 
shopkeeper has the bearing of someone who knows weapons intimately.

'Looking for something specific?' he asks, not unkindly."

Player: "I need a good sword and some leather armor."

LLM: "He sets down the sword he was polishing. 'I can help with both. For 
weapons, I have shortswords, longswords, hand axes... any preference? As for 
armor, I work with leather regularly - how much protection are you looking for?'

What's your preference?"
```

**Bad (Over-Generation):**
```
Player: "We need weapons and armor."

LLM: [Generates 50+ items, lists every weapon and armor piece in detail, 
describes entire shop layout, gives detailed background of every item...]
```

## üéÅ BONUS: CUSTOM COMMISSIONS

Many craftspeople offer custom work:

**Timeline:**
- Simple items: 1-3 days
- Quality items: 1-2 weeks
- Masterwork items: 1-4 weeks
- Complex items: 1-3 months

**Cost Adjustment:**
- Custom work: +25-50% over standard
- Masterwork: +50-100% over standard
- Rush job: +25-50% additional fee

**Process:**
1. Customer describes what they want
2. Craftsperson discusses requirements
3. Agree on timeline and cost
4. Pay deposit (50%)
5. Item created during campaign
6. Pay balance and collect
