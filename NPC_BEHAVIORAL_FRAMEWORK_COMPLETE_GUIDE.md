## ðŸ“– NPC FUNDAMENTALS

### What Is an NPC?
A Non-Player Character controlled by the PM. Anyone not a player character.
- May be friendly, hostile, or neutral
- Has personality, goals, secrets
- Evolves through campaign
- Remembers player actions
- Has relationships with other NPCs

### NPC Scope
**Who counts as NPC?**
- Settlement inhabitants (shopkeepers, nobles, workers)
- Combat enemies (bandits, monsters, creatures)
- Quest givers (employers, mystery figures)
- Allies (companions, hired help)
- Rivals (enemies, obstacles)
- Mysterious strangers (travelers, wanderers)

### Core NPC Elements
Every NPC needs:
1. **Name** (memorable, pronounceable)
2. **Role/Occupation** (what they do)
3. **Personality** (how they behave)
4. **Goal** (what they want)
5. **Flaw/Secret** (complication)
6. **Relationship to party** (how they view players)
7. **Stat profile** (if combat-relevant)

**Core Traits:**
- Confident, commanding
- Takes charge naturally
- Decision maker
- Often stubborn
- Inspires loyalty

**Motivations:**
- Power, influence, respect
- Organizational control
- Achievement, legacy

- Declarative statements
- Uses "we" frequently
- Gives orders naturally
- Confident tone
- Direct eye contact

**Conflict Style:**
- Confrontational
- Dominates room
- Negotiates from strength
- Rarely backs down
- Competitive

**Example NPCs:**

- Colonel Vex (military commander)
- Captain Heron (guard leader)

- Make leadership decisions
- Push party toward action
- Take credit for successes
- Blame others for failures

## ðŸ’­ PERSONALITY DEPTH LAYERS

### Layer 1: Surface Personality
**What people immediately see:**
- Friendly or standoffish
- Talkative or quiet
- Confident or nervous
- Organized or chaotic

Example: "Bella is warm and outgoing."

### Layer 2: Core Motivations
**What drives deep behavior:**
- What they want (often hidden)
- What they fear (often hidden)
- What they value (sometimes hidden)
- What they avoid (often hidden)

Example: "Bella wants to be needed; she fears abandonment."

### Layer 3: Secrets/Flaws
**What complicates them:**
- Hidden past
- Ongoing problems
- Moral compromises
- Hidden relationships

Example: "Bella has a hidden child she gave up; she drinks to cope."

### Layer 4: Growth/Change
**How they evolve:**
- How party interactions change them
- How experiences affect them
- What realizations they have
- How they grow throughout campaign

Example: "After party helps her, Bella finally reconnects with her daughter."

## ðŸ”— RELATIONSHIP MECHANICS

### Relationship Stages

**Stage 1: Stranger** (Initial meeting)
- No relationship
- Neutral stance
- Testing each other
- Default assumptions
- Duration: Minutes to hours

**Stage 2: Acquaintance** (After interaction)
- Light relationship
- Basic information shared
- Tentative trust
- Forming opinions
- Duration: Hours to days

**Stage 3: Associate** (Regular contact)
- Established relationship
- Willing to work together
- Know basic motivations
- Moderate trust
- Duration: Days to weeks

**Stage 4: Ally** (Demonstrated loyalty)
- Strong positive relationship
- Trust each other
- Will help in danger
- Coordinated action
- Duration: Weeks to months

**Stage 5: Friend** (Deep connection)
- Very strong relationship
- Trust completely
- Personal support
- Will sacrifice for each other
- Duration: Months to years

**Stage 6: Beloved** (Deepest bond)
- Romantic/family-level connection
- Complete trust
- Life priority
- Mutual dependence
- Duration: Years to lifetime

### Relationship Modifiers

**Actions that improve relationships:**
- Help in need: +2 points
- Honest conversation: +1 point
- Shared experience: +2 points
- Defending them: +3 points
- Material gift: +1 point
- Completing their quest: +2 points
- Remembering personal details: +1 point

**Actions that damage relationships:**
- Betrayal: -5 points
- Dishonesty revealed: -2 points
- Refusing help: -1 point
- Insulting beliefs: -3 points
- Hurting loved ones: -4 points
- Breaking promises: -2 points
- Theft/deception: -2 points

**Relationship Point System:**
- 0-3 points: Stranger/Acquaintance
- 4-7 points: Associate
- 8-12 points: Ally
- 13-17 points: Friend
- 18+ points: Beloved

### Relationship Effects

**How relationships affect NPC behavior:**

| Relationship | Will do | Won't do | Attitude |
|-------------|---------|----------|----------|
| Stranger | Minimal | Dangerous/costly | Neutral, suspicious |
| Acquaintance | Basic tasks | Risky/dangerous | Polite, cautious |
| Associate | Normal jobs | Extremely risky | Friendly, cooperative |
| Ally | Most jobs | Personal sacrifice | Supportive, helpful |
| Friend | Almost anything | Betray principles | Warm, trusting |
| Beloved | Anything, sacrifice | Betray self/family | Devoted, passionate |

## ðŸŽ¯ NPC INTERACTION FRAMEWORK

### First Meeting Protocol

**Step 1: Assessment (1-3 seconds)**
- NPCs assess party appearance, weapons, demeanor
- Make snap judgments based on archetype
- Decide initial stance

**Step 2: Opening Interaction**
- NPCs use their speech pattern naturally
- Show their personality immediately
- Test party reaction

**Step 3: Establishing Relationship**
- NPCs decide if party is threat, opportunity, or neither
- Adjust behavior based on party response
- Begin building relationship points

**Step 4: Exchanging Information**
- NPCs share as much or little as their personality allows
- Ask questions matching their archetype
- Reveal secrets only if trust established

### NPC Response to Party Actions

**Party acts honorably:**
- Idealist NPCs: +3 points, strong approval
- Leader NPCs: +1 point, respect shown
- Rogue NPCs: Suspicious (-1 point, see what's in it for them)
- Caregiver NPCs: +2 points, happy to help

**Party acts dishonestly:**
- Idealist NPCs: -5 points, moral outrage
- Sage NPCs: -2 points, disappointment
- Lover NPCs: -3 points, betrayed feeling
- Rogue NPCs: +1 point, respect for cleverness

**Party acts powerfully (combat skill shown):**
- Leader NPCs: +2 points, respect competence
- Craftsperson NPCs: +1 point, appreciate skill
- Everyman NPCs: +1 point, impressed
- Innocent NPCs: +1 point, feel safer

### Memory & Evolution

**NPCs Remember:**
- Major party actions (always)
- How party treated them (affects relationship)
- Information shared (might spread rumors)
- Promises made (will recall if broken)
- Injuries/help received (creates lasting bond)

**NPCs Change:**
- Over campaign arc
- Based on party influence
- Through plot developments
- In response to major events
- As relationships deepen
