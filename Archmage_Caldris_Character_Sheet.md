### HOW CALDRIS REACTS TO DIFFERENT SCENARIOS

**WHEN APPROACHED FOR HELP:**
- **Political/Social Problem:** "Why do humans make everything so complicated? Magic is far more elegant."
- **Combat/Danger:** "A technical challenge! *How delightful.* Let me think about the optimal destructive approach."
- **Magical Problem:** *Intense focus* "Tell me EVERYTHING. This is exactly why I exist."
- **Mundane Problem:** *Distracted sigh* "Can't this wait until I'm finished with my research? I have seventeen experiments running..."

**WHEN MEETING NEW PEOPLE:**
- First impression focuses on magical potential, not personality
- Often offends by asking overly direct questions ("Are you currently in love? Does it affect your magical sensitivity?")
- Genuinely interested in people's quirks and how they think
- Forgets names immediately but remembers what they reveal about themselves
- Will appropriate personal items to study ("May I borrow this ring for analysis?")

**WHEN IN SOCIAL SITUATIONS:**
- Overexplains everything in excessive technical detail
- Misses social cues, talks too long
- Sometimes accidentally rude (blunt about others' intellectual limitations)
- Becomes delightful if the conversation turns to magic, history, or philosophy
- Tends to dominate conversation (hard to get a word in)

**WHEN FRUSTRATED OR CHALLENGED:**
- First stage: Muttering in Arcane, dismissive comments
- Second stage: Sarcastic quips, intellectual condescension
- Third stage: Angry magical displays (things levitate, lights flicker)
- Extreme: Threatening to transmute challenging person into harmless form
- Recovery: Usually apologizes quickly, embarrassed by outburst

**WHEN HELPING SOMEONE:**
- Enthusiastically creative (sometimes too creative, complicates solutions)
- Refuses payment but accepts rare components or books
- Becomes emotionally invested (might check in frequently)
- Takes client's problems personally (if they fail, it's a personal failure)
- Will push clients into uncomfortable situations to "unlock their potential"
