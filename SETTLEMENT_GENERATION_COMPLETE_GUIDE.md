## üìñ SETTLEMENT FUNDAMENTALS

### What Is a Settlement?
A permanent or semi-permanent human habitation with:
- Population (5 villagers to 50,000+ city inhabitants)
- Buildings (homes, shops, fortifications, temples)
- Governance (lord, mayor, council, clan leader)
- Resources (farms, mines, forests, water, trade)
- NPCs (inhabitants with personalities, routines, relationships)
- Encounters (potential combat, social, exploration situations)

### Settlement Types (Medieval European Fantasy)

| Type | Size | Population | Examples | Typical Location |
|------|------|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Hamlet** | Tiny | 5-25 | Farmstead, logging camp | Deep forest, mountain pass |
| **Village** | Small | 25-150 | Farming community | River valley, forest edge |
| **Town** | Medium | 150-500 | Trade hub, mining town | Major crossroads, river |
| **City** | Large | 500-5,000 | Capital, major port | Coast, large river, trade route |
| **Metropolis** | Huge | 5,000-50,000+ | Kingdom capital | Major river junction, coast |

## üèòÔ∏è HAMLET GENERATION (5-25 people)

### Hamlet Overview
Smallest settlement type. Usually family-owned farmsteads, logging camps, or hermitage villages. Isolated, self-sufficient, rarely visited by outsiders.

### Core Components

**Population Distribution:**
- 1-4 main families (5-20 people)
- 0-5 travelers/temporary residents
- Rarely any hired help
- Everyone knows everyone

**Buildings (Typical Hamlet):**
- 2-4 wooden houses
- 1 small storehouse/granary
- 1 well or water source
- 1 communal fire pit
- Maybe 1 small shrine

**Governance:**
- Eldest family member is "leader"
- Decisions made by family council
- No formal government
- Disputes settled by consensus

**Resources:**
- Small farm (2-4 acres)
- Goat/sheep herd (5-15 animals)
- Chickens (3-8)
- Well-maintained water source
- Maybe honey bees
- Wood source (forest nearby)

### HAMLET EXAMPLE 1: Logging Camp (Mountain Foothills)

**Name:** Timber Hollow

**Location:** Eastern mountain foothills, within 2 days of a major river

**Population:** 12 people

- Woodshed (logs, tools, equipment)

**Daily Activity:**
- Morning: Men go logging, gather wood
- Midday: Rest, sharpen tools, eat
- Afternoon: More logging or tool repair
- Evening: Blacksmith works (heating/cooling)

**Resources:**

1. **Bandits want protection money** (Task: Intimidate or fight)
2. **Wolves attacking at night** (Combat: 3-5 wolves)
3. **Wandering adventurers need work** (Social: hire for logging/security)
4. **Wild boar defense needed** (Combat: 1 large boar)
5. **Blacksmith needs rare ore** (Quest: find specific mineral)

- Hunting trophies (boar tusks, wolf pelts)

**PM Notes:**
- Isolated but peaceful community
- Good starting point for new adventurers
- Jobs available (security, heavy lifting, escorting)

## üèûÔ∏è VILLAGE GENERATION (25-150 people)

### Village Overview
Small farming community. Population centers around agriculture. Market day brings surrounding settlements. Local lord or mayor governs. Mix of families, craftspeople, merchants, travelers.

### Core Components

**Population Distribution:**
- 15-30 farming families (70-120 people)
- Craftspeople (blacksmith, carpenter, miller) (3-5)
- Merchants/traders (1-3)
- Clergy (1-2)
- Travelers/newcomers (2-5)
- Adventurers/soldiers (0-3)

**Buildings (Typical Village):**
- 20-40 houses
- 1-2 grain stores/granaries
- 1 mill (water mill or hand mill)
- 1 tavern/inn
- 1 blacksmith shop
- 1 general store
- 1 small temple/shrine
- 1 well + water distribution
- Maybe 1 small garrison/watchtower
- Maybe 1 healer's cottage

**Governance:**
- Village Elder or Lord's representative
- Town council (3-7 members)
- Formal disputes handled by council
- Market regulations
- Tax collection (to lord or local use)

**Resources:**
- Large communal farm (50-100 acres)
- Individual family farms (2-5 acres each)
- Livestock (communal herd, individual animals)
- Mill (process grain)
- Forest access (hunting, wood, herbs)
- Water source (river, stream, or wells)

**Layout Pattern (Medieval European):**
- Town center (market square, well, gathering point)
- Main road (usually north-south for trade)
- Residential areas (radiating from center)
- Church/shrine (often central or elevated)
- Craftspeople district (blacksmith, mill, market)
- Outer farms (gradually to forest/wilderness edge)

### VILLAGE EXAMPLE 1: Agricultural Hub (River Valley)

**Name:** Millbrook Village

**Location:** River valley, crossroads between two major roads, 20 miles from capital

**Population:** 87 people

**Physical Layout:**
- Market square (30x40 feet, center of village)
- Main road running north-south (connects to major roads)
- Residential district west (25 houses)
- Farm district east (open fields, 60 acres)
- Mill house (on river, water-powered)
- Tavern/Inn "The Crossed Plows" (market square)
- General store (market square)
- Blacksmith shop (near center)
- Temple of agriculture god (elevated, north)
- Guard house (small garrison, 3 soldiers)
- Granary (storage, east side)
- Water well (market square, backup)

**Resources:**
- River (water power, fishing, transport)
- Farmland (grain, vegetables)
- Livestock (sheep herd 40, goats 20, cattle 8)
- Forest (2 miles east, hunting, wood)
- Stone (quarry 4 miles north)
- Mills (grain processing, flour production)

**Governance:**
- Village Elder: Hadrik (70 years old, fair, respected)
- Council: 5 members (miller, blacksmith, senior farmer, merchant, cleric)
- 3 soldiers (enforcement, security)
- Taxes: 1 silver per family per season (goes to regional lord)

**Trade & Economy:**
- Primary: Grain, flour, wheat
- Secondary: Wool, leather, metalwork
- Exports: Flour (to capital), wool (to market)
- Imports: Salt, luxury goods, tools, books
- Market day: Every 5 days (farmers sell, merchants arrive)

**Daily Routine:**
- Dawn: Farmers work fields
- Midday: Market activity (if market day)
- Afternoon: Crafting, maintenance, hunting
- Evening: Tavern socializing
- Night: Rest, guard duty (3 soldiers rotate)

**Encounters:**

1. **Bandits harassing traders** (Combat/Quest: stop bandit gang)
2. **Crop blight threat** (Investigation: find cause, fix disease)
3. **Raiders from north** (Combat: defend village)
4. **Merchant caravan assassination** (Investigation: who benefits?)
5. **Strange disappearances** (Investigation: kidnapping? monster?)
6. **Tax collector arrives** (Social: political intrigue)
7. **Traveling carnival** (Social: entertainment, potential theft)
8. **Plague symptoms** (Investigation: quarantine? cure?)

**NPCs & Personalities:**

- **Hadrik (Elder, 70s):** Respected, fair, concerned about village, conservative decisions
- **Einar (Miller):** Shrewd businessman, controls grain trade, competitive, wealthy
- **Brenna (Blacksmith):** Skilled, impatient, excellent gossip source, flirtiest NPC
- **Father Aldwyn (Cleric):** Holy man, good with people, secret drinking problem
- **Maris (Merchant):** Sharp trader, traveling merchant, brings news/goods, secretive
- **Yuri (Young farmer):** Hot-headed, ambitious, challenges traditions
- **Renna (Innkeeper):** Warm, excellent food, knows everything, grandmother figure
- **Guard Captain Vex:** Competent, fair, bored with peaceful life, wants adventure

**Treasure Potential:**
- Merchant caravan goods (300-500 gold worth)
- Stolen tax money (100 gold hidden)
- Blacksmith's special commissions (fine weapons worth 50-100 gold)
- Temple donations (50 gold in funds)
- Bandit loot (if cleared)

**Local Quests:**

1. **Investigate crop blight** (Difficulty: Average to Tough)
   - Task: Find source, apply solution
   - Reward: Gratitude, free lodging, 20 gold
   - Failure: Village faces famine

2. **Guard merchant caravan to capital** (Difficulty: Average)
   - Task: Protect goods for 3-day journey
   - Reward: 30 gold + provisions
   - Complication: Bandits ambush

3. **Clear bandit camp** (Difficulty: Tough to Very Difficult)
   - Task: Raid camp, eliminate leader
   - Reward: 50+ gold, permanent gratitude
   - Complication: More bandits than expected

4. **Find missing persons** (Difficulty: Average)
   - Task: Investigate disappearances, find whereabouts
   - Reward: 25 gold, information
   - Complication: Leads to larger conspiracy

5. **Heal village healer** (Difficulty: Tough)
   - Task: Find rare herb or cure for poison
   - Reward: Free healing, village support
   - Complication: Herb is in dangerous location

**PM Notes:**
- Good starting point for medium-length campaigns
- Multiple quest hooks available
- Local politics can create complications
- Good mix of combat and social encounters
- Realistic medieval village feels lived-in
- Players can develop relationships with NPCs

## üèôÔ∏è TOWN GENERATION (150-500 people)

### Town Overview
Significant settlement. Trading hub, possibly with castle/lord's residence. Organized districts. Significant craft and trade presence. Multiple taverns, inns, temples. Functioning government with organized guard/military.

### Core Components

**Population Distribution:**
- Merchants/traders (40-80 people)
- Craftspeople (60-100): blacksmiths, carpenters, millers, bakers, masons
- Service workers (40-80): tavern staff, servants, porters
- Military/guards (15-30)
- Clergy (3-8)
- Administrative (2-5): clerks, record-keepers
- Laborers (30-60): general work
- Nobles/wealthy (5-15): lord's family, rich merchants
- Traveling visitors (10-20 at any time)

**Buildings (Typical Town):**
- 80-150 residential buildings
- 30-50 shops/markets
- 5-10 taverns/inns
- 3-5 temples
- 2-4 craft halls (blacksmith, carpenter, miller)
- 1 market hall (central)
- 1 courthouse/government building
- 1 garrison/barracks
- 1 stable (large, 40+ horses)
- 2-3 granaries
- 1-2 guest houses
- Library/archive (possible)
- Walls (usually wooden palisade or stone for larger towns)

**Governance:**
- Town Lord or appointed Mayor
- Town Council (5-15 members)
- Guard Captain commanding garrison
- Guild Masters (one per major craft)
- Market Master (trade regulation)
- Formal laws and enforcement

**Districts (Typical Organization):**
- Market district (central, commercial)
- Noble/government district (often elevated or central)
- Craft district (blacksmith, carpenter, mason)
- Trade/warehouse district (goods storage)
- Temple district (religious buildings)
- Residential districts (various wealth levels)
- Outer areas (less developed, working class)
