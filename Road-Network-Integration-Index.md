## ROAD SPECIFICATIONS & STANDARDS

### Universal Road Standards

**Width:** 15-20 feet (accommodates two wagons side-by-side)  
**Surface Variations:**
- **Primary routes:** Mixed stone-paved and packed earth
- **Secondary routes:** Primarily packed earth, some stone sections
- **Frontier routes:** Variable; primarily packed earth and cleared paths

**Maintenance Standards:**

| Route Classification | Maintenance Level | Authority | Patrol Frequency |
|---------------------|-------------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Primary (East Road) | Well-maintained | Shared | Daily |
| Major (Northern Trade Road) | Well-maintained | Shared | 2-3x weekly |
| Secondary (Western Branch, Spur) | Good condition | Shared | Weekly |
| Frontier (Southern Route) | Irregular | Frontier | Irregular |

### Seasonal Impact Modifiers

**Spring:** +20-30% travel time (mud, water levels)  
**Summer:** Baseline (optimal conditions)  
**Autumn:** Baseline (good conditions)  
**Winter:** +50-100% travel time (ice, snow, hazards)

| Regional streams | Western/Spur | Low | Minimal | Safe |

### Primary Hazard Zones

1. **Blackstone Ravine (East Road, 1.2 mi)**
   - Hazard: Goblin territory boundary, goblin scouts, damaged bridge, complex terrain
   - Mitigation: Militia checkpoint, repair work ongoing
   - Safety Level: Medium (daytime safer than night)
   - Recommendation: Travel in daylight, avoid lingering

2. **Goblin Hills Approach (Southern Route, 3.0 mi)**
   - Hazard: Goblin activity, frontier wilderness, limited militia presence
   - Mitigation: Militia outposts, scout reports
   - Safety Level: Medium-High (adventure location)
   - Recommendation: Experienced travelers only; militia coordination suggested

3. **Wilderness Transitions (Spur & Western routes, 3-4 mi)**
   - Hazard: Wildlife, weather exposure, reduced civilization
   - Mitigation: Established paths, regular traffic
   - Safety Level: Low-Medium (manageable for standard travelers)
   - Recommendation: Standard precautions adequate
