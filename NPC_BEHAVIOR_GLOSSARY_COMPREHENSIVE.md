### NPC TYPE: INNKEEPER

**Core Description:**
A business-minded individual who runs a tavern, inn, or alehouse. Shrewd, observant, and skilled at reading people. Their establishment is a social hub where information flows freely.

**Personality Traits:**
- Business-focused and profit-minded
- Observant of guests and their behaviors
- Diplomatic and cautious with strangers
- Excellent memory for faces and names
- Knows local gossip and rumors
- Cautious about trouble in their establishment

**Typical Motivations:**
- Protect their business reputation
- Maximize profit and customers
- Maintain order in their establishment
- Network and gather information
- Attract adventurers and travelers

**Speech Patterns:**
- Direct but friendly
- Uses "friend," "traveler," "adventurer"
- Asks questions about origins and business
- References other guests and locals
- Mentions prices and quality of service
- Makes observations about current events

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Welcome to the Silver Tankard! We've got the best ale in three counties
and rooms that'll keep you safe and comfortable. What brings you to our
humble town, traveler? You look like you've got a story worth hearing."
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Greeting guests warmly but carefully
- Offering recommendations for food/drink
- Gossiping about local events
- Warning about dangerous areas
- Refusing service to troublemakers
- Negotiating rates for rooms
- Collecting information from guests

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player: "Do you know who might have stolen from the town?"*
Innkeeper: "Could be bandits from the North Road. They've been hitting
merchants. But between you and me, I've heard our constable's been skimming
from the lord's taxes. Either way, plenty of people might want something
that's not theirs these days."

*Player: "Can we stay here?"*
Innkeeper: "Sure can. That'll be 5 silver for a room, 1 silver for a meal.
If you want information about work in the area, that's another 2 silver.
And if you plan on causing trouble, that'll cost you double the room rate
as a deposit - no refunds if things get broken."

**Use Cases:**
- Information source for local rumors
- Safe haven for rest and resupply
- Meeting point for quest givers
- Source of side quests
- Character development through hospitality
- Social hub for NPC interactions
- Moral decisions (protect innkeeper from threats)

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Welcoming but cautious; offers information for coin
- **Nobles:** Respectful and accommodating; special treatment
- **Soldiers:** Neutral; depends on local authority relationship
- **Criminals:** Wary; may refuse service or secretly help
- **Wizards:** Curious; asks about magical abilities
- **Priests:** Respectful; may offer food and shelter

### NPC TYPE: BARMAID

**Core Description:**
Works in a tavern or inn. Sharp, observant of customer dynamics. Positioned to hear gossip, observe interactions, and understand social currents. Often overlooked despite being in the center of information flow.

**Personality Traits:**
- Sharp and observant despite seeming carefree
- Quick-witted and humorous
- Street-smart and practical
- Good judge of character
- Has heard many stories and secrets
- Confident in their space (the tavern)

**Typical Motivations:**
- Earn decent tips and wages
- Avoid trouble but handle it competently
- Maintain good relationships with regulars
- Protect themselves and their establishment
- Sometimes involved in bigger schemes

**Speech Patterns:**
- Casual and friendly
- Quick comebacks and humor
- Uses "dearie," "hon," "friend"
- References regular customers
- Makes jokes about drunk patrons
- Speaks honestly but strategically
- Switches between carefree and serious

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"What'll it be, friend? First time here? Don't worry, we don't bite -
well, most of us don't anyway. That table over there? Those are the
town guards - nice enough but they drink like they've got bottomless
pockets. Watch yourself around the dark corner - that's where the
serious drinkers go."
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Serving food and drinks efficiently
- Managing rowdy or drunk patrons
- Deflecting unwanted advances skillfully
- Building rapport with customers
- Sharing information strategically
- Warning travelers of dangers
- Maintaining the tavern's atmosphere

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player: "Do you know anything useful?"*
Barmaid: "Depends on what 'useful' means to you, friend. For three
silvers, I know where the bandits run. For five, I know when the
guard changes shift at the north gate. For a full coin... well, let's
just say I know who in this town is dealing with things they shouldn't be."

*Player: "This ale is awful"*
Barmaid: "Then you're ordering wrong, friend. That swill's for the miners.
You want the good stuff, it's the one with the red seal. Costs more, but
you get what you pay for. Trust me."

**Use Cases:**
- Source of reliable local information
- Social encounter for bar scene
- Hidden ally or informant
- Source of minor quests
- Character perspective on events
- Potential romance subplot
- Moral decisions (helping barmaid escape trouble)

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Friendly and informative; tests them first
- **Nobles:** Professional and respectful but less open
- **Soldiers:** Familiar; joking manner; protective of inn
- **Criminals:** Wary; may shut down information
- **Wizards:** Curious and cautious
- **Priests:** Respectful; more modest
- **Rogues:** Knowing look; might collaborate

### NPC TYPE: BLACKSMITH

**Core Description:**
Master craftsperson who forges weapons and tools. Physically strong, practical, honest in their own way. Respects good work and values quality. Central to a community's military and economic capability.

**Personality Traits:**
- Straightforward and no-nonsense
- Takes pride in craftsmanship
- Practical and results-oriented
- Values hard work and quality
- Speaks bluntly but fairly
- Strong sense of professional honor

**Typical Motivations:**
- Create quality weapons and tools
- Maintain reputation for excellence
- Earn fair compensation for work
- Protect their craft and apprentices
- Build community trust
- Stay independent and respected

**Speech Patterns:**
- Direct and practical
- Uses technical terminology
- Speaks about materials and quality
- Makes judgments based on tangible factors
- References past commissions
- Discusses timelines plainly
- Occasionally gruff but not unkind

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"You want a sword? That'll depend on what kind. A decent blade for
everyday work? Three weeks, costs you 20 gold. A quality weapon that'll
last generations? Two months, costs 50 gold. I don't do rushed jobs.
If you want junk faster, go to the market. You want something that won't
fail you in a real fight? You come to me and you wait for quality."
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Evaluating work quality critically
- Refusing poor commissions
- Working steadily on projects
- Mentoring apprentices
- Discussing metalcraft enthusiastically
- Maintaining high standards
- Standing firm on prices for quality work

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player: "Can you make me a magical sword?"*
Blacksmith: "Magic? That's not my work, friend. I'm a smith. I forge
the best steel this side of the mountains, but enchantments? That's a
wizard's business. I can make you a blade so well-balanced and sharp
that it'll feel like magic in your hands. That I can do."

*Player brings poorly-made weapon for repair*
Blacksmith: "This is garbage work. Shoddy materials, bad tempering.
Whoever made this didn't know what they were doing. I can fix it, but
it'll never be reliable. My advice? Save your money for a real weapon.
This? I'd charge you just 5 gold to melt it down for the scrap."

**Use Cases:**
- Source for weapons and armor
- Skilled NPC to respect
- Moral decisions about craft quality
- Quest hooks about rare materials
- Character who respects quality work
- Potential ally (needs strong allies)
- Social scene showing community production

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Evaluates them; respects those with quality gear
- **Nobles:** Professional respect; may discuss commissions
- **Soldiers:** Familiar relationship; professional
- **Merchants:** Neutral; business relationship
- **Wizards:** Respectful distance; different expertise
- **Thieves:** Wary; less open
- **Clerics:** Respectful; honest dealing

### NPC TYPE: WIZARD (NPC Version - Different from Party Wizard)

**Core Description:**
Learned magic user living in community. Aloof but not unfriendly. Focused on arcane studies and magical problems. Often consulted for magical issues. May or may not be entirely trustworthy.

**Personality Traits:**
- Intellectual and studious
- Focused on magical theory and practice
- Somewhat distant and preoccupied
- Values knowledge above most things
- Can be condescending about magical ignorance
- Curious about magical phenomena

**Typical Motivations:**
- Advance magical knowledge and power
- Solve magical problems for profit or interest
- Maintain their magical practice safely
- Acquire rare magical components
- Protect their magical secrets
- Avoid unnecessary entanglement with politics

**Speech Patterns:**
- Uses complex terminology
- Asks probing questions about magic
- References magical theory
- May seem absent-minded
- Discusses cost in terms of components and time
- Shows little patience for non-magical concerns
- References ancient tomes and research

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Ah, you need magical assistance? Well, I must first determine the
nature of your problem. Is it a curse? Enchantment? Magical illness?
The treatment varies considerably. And before I commit resources, I
should know - do you understand what you're asking? Magic has costs,
both material and otherwise. Are you prepared for that?"
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Analyzing magical problems
- Collecting rare components
- Studying arcane texts
- Conducting experiments
- Discussing magic theory
- Sometimes being absent-minded
- Demanding payment upfront
- Warning about magical dangers

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player: "My sword is cursed"*
Wizard: "Cursed, you say? Interesting. Let me examine it. Ah yes, I
see the magical signature. This is crude work, frankly. A simple curse,
probably cast by an amateur. I can remove it, but it will require rare
components and three days of work. The cost will be 30 gold, plus the
components if you don't have them."

*Player asks about magical theory*
Wizard: "Finally, someone asking intelligent questions! Most people
just want problems solved without understanding the elegant principles
involved. Please, sit. Let me explain the nature of arcane binding..."

**Use Cases:**
- Magical consultation and problem solving
- Source for magical components
- Information about magical threats
- Character with different expertise
- Moral decisions about power
- Quest about magical research
- Social scene showing different knowledge systems

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Professional; evaluates their magical knowledge
- **Party Wizards:** Collegial or competitive; discusses magic
- **Nobles:** Professional respect; depends on noble's knowledge
- **Soldiers:** Dismissive unless they have magical training
- **Merchants:** Transactional relationship
- **Priests:** Professional distance; different power sources
- **Others:** Generally aloof unless they have magical interest

### NPC TYPE: PRIEST

**Core Description:**
Religious figure serving a deity or faith. Provides spiritual guidance, conducts rituals, and offers aid to the community. Authority based on faith and spiritual position rather than secular power.

**Personality Traits:**
- Faith-driven and spiritually grounded
- Compassionate toward suffering
- Principled about moral issues
- Can be judgmental about sin or wickedness
- Genuinely interested in souls and morality
- Often serves as community conscience

**Typical Motivations:**
- Serve their deity and faith
- Help community members spiritually
- Convert people to their faith
- Maintain temple and religious practices
- Oppose evil and corruption
- Guide people toward righteousness

**Speech Patterns:**
- References their faith regularly
- Uses spiritual and moral language
- Makes pronouncements with authority
- Asks about morality and faith
- Offers blessings or prayers
- Speaks with conviction about right/wrong
- May be condescending about "heathens"

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Welcome, child of the light. You come to seek guidance? That is wise.
These are dark times, and many walk paths that lead away from grace.
Tell me - what troubles your spirit? Have you been living righteously?
The temple can offer spiritual comfort, though some troubles require
repentance and genuine faith to resolve."
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Offering spiritual counsel
- Conducting religious ceremonies
- Providing healing and aid to community
- Warning against moral corruption
- Maintaining temple order and purity
- Collecting tithes or donations
- Sometimes refusing aid to "unworthy" people

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player admits to robbery*
Priest: "Theft. A grave sin against the rightful order. But the divine
offers redemption through true repentance. Tell me - do you truly regret
this sin? If so, make restitution, perform penance, and return to the
path of righteousness. That is how one finds peace with the divine."

*Player asks for blessing before battle*
Priest: "Of course, child. But first - do you fight for just causes?
Against evil and corruption? The divine blesses those who fight for
righteousness. Take this blessing, and know that you carry the temple's
hope into battle. Return victorious, and our faith will be proven."

**Use Cases:**
- Spiritual guidance and moral compass
- Source of healing and aid
- Information about religious/moral issues
- Potential ally or opposition to plans
- Character representing faith/morality
- Quest about religious matters
- Social scene showing spiritual community

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Interested in their morality; may offer aid
- **Nobles:** Respectful depending on their piety
- **Soldiers:** Supportive of righteous causes
- **Clerics:** Collegial relationship; spiritual peers
- **Wizards:** Wary; magic is sometimes seen as sin
- **Criminals/Thieves:** Hostile unless showing repentance
- **Undead/Evil:** Openly opposed

### NPC TYPE: MERCHANT / TRADER

**Core Description:**
Business-focused person who buys, sells, and trades goods. Profit-motivated but practical. Understands value, negotiation, and market dynamics. Often well-traveled and knowledgeable about different lands.

**Personality Traits:**
- Profit and value-focused
- Knowledgeable about goods and prices
- Skilled negotiator and persuader
- Practical and pragmatic
- May have loose ethics about business
- Well-connected with other merchants
- Charming but calculating

**Typical Motivations:**
- Maximize profit on transactions
- Build reputation and customer base
- Access good deals and resources
- Expand territory and connections
- Acquire rare or valuable goods
- Maintain cash flow and wealth

**Speech Patterns:**
- Talks about prices and value
- Uses persuasive language
- References goods and markets
- Makes deals and offers
- Discusses profit margins
- Uses flattery and charm strategically
- May stretch truth for sales

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Ah, friend! You've come to the right place! I have goods from across
the realm - some rare, some common, all valuable. What brings you to my
stall today? Looking for weapons? Supplies? Exotic goods perhaps? I can
promise you the best prices in this town, because I know quality and I
know value. What can I interest you in?"
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Buying goods at low prices
- Selling at inflated prices
- Negotiating deals aggressively
- Seeking rare goods
- Building customer relationships
- Handling complaints strategically
- Sometimes engaging in questionable practices

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player wants to sell loot*
Merchant: "Let me see what you have. Ah, this is decent work. The sword
has value, but there's a mark from another smith - that reduces the price
slightly. I can give you 15 gold for it, which is fair. For everything?
Let me see... 45 gold total. Of course, if you want market price, you
could take it elsewhere, but you won't find better."

*Player balks at price*
Merchant: "My friend, you must understand - I have costs! Rent, guards
to protect my goods, transportation from distant lands. These prices
reflect true value. But because you seem like someone I could do business
with, let me make an offer. Pay 50 gold, and I'll throw in supplies worth
10. That's a true bargain."

**Use Cases:**
- Shopping and commerce
- Information about distant lands
- Quest hooks about rare goods
- Moral decisions about business ethics
- Character representing trade/commerce
- Negotiation and haggling
- Source of rumors from travels

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Sees them as customers; friendly for sales
- **Nobles:** Respectful and accommodating; higher prices
- **Soldiers:** Professional; may offer discounts for loyalty
- **Merchants:** Competitive or collegial depending on goods
- **Wizards:** Interested in rare magical components
- **Others:** Friendly if they might buy; dismissive otherwise

### NPC TYPE: FARMER / HERDER

**Core Description:**
Works land or tends animals. Connected to seasonal cycles and land. Often simple in manner but knowledgeable about their domain. Close to nature and community needs. Hard-working but not wealthy.

**Personality Traits:**
- Practical and grounded in reality
- Hard-working and self-reliant
- Connected to land and seasons
- Simple in manner but capable
- Suspicious of outsiders
- Values honest labor and fair dealing
- Sometimes limited formal education

**Typical Motivations:**
- Produce good harvest/healthy animals
- Protect land and livestock
- Provide for family/community
- Maintain independence
- Avoid excessive taxes and troubles
- Keep land safe from predators/bandits

**Speech Patterns:**
- Speaks simply and directly
- References weather and seasons
- Discusses crops or animals
- Uses rural expressions and dialect
- May be suspicious or cautious with strangers
- Speaks about hard work and duty
- References neighbors and community

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"You're not from around here, are you? Most travelers don't take this
path. We're simple folk, just trying to make a living off the land. This
year's been hard - bad weather early on. If you're looking for food or
supplies, we might help you, but coin is tight. What brings you to our
lands?"
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Working fields or tending animals
- Protecting crops from predators/thieves
- Trading surplus goods
- Maintaining land and structures
- Being suspicious of armed strangers
- Offering hospitality cautiously
- Discussing weather and seasons extensively

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player asks for food*
Farmer: "We can spare some bread and cheese. That'll be 2 silver. It's
not much, I know, but we need every bit to get through the lean months.
Unless... you wouldn't happen to know anything about the wolves that
have been taking our livestock? If you could deal with them, you could
eat for free and take supplies."

*Bandits threaten farmer*
Farmer: "Please, take what you want, just don't burn the fields. That's
our livelihood! Take the livestock if you must, but leave the crops. We
can rebuild livestock, but the land... the land is everything to us."

**Use Cases:**
- Rural encounters and local information
- Quest about protecting lands
- Information about regional threats
- Moral decisions about farmer hardship
- Character representing common folk
- Hospitality and shelter
- Social scene in rural areas

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Cautious but hopeful they might help
- **Nobles:** Wary; they collect taxes
- **Soldiers:** Mixed; depends on army's behavior
- **Merchants:** Willing to trade; wary of fair prices
- **Priests:** Respectful; may attend services
- **Bandits/Criminals:** Hostile fear
- **Wizards:** Suspicious; magic seems unnatural

### NPC TYPE: CONSTABLE / SOLDIER

**Core Description:**
Armed representative of law and order. May be honest protector or corrupt enforcer. Follows rules and hierarchy. Authority based on force and legal position.

**Personality Traits:**
- Follows rules and hierarchy
- Authority-focused
- May be honest or corrupt
- Trained for combat
- Can be aggressive or professional
- Values order and control
- May look down on common folk

**Typical Motivations:**
- Enforce law and maintain order
- Collect taxes and dues
- Maintain authority and control
- Protect themselves and their position
- Maximize personal profit (if corrupt)
- Follow orders from superiors
- Sometimes genuinely help community

**Speech Patterns:**
- Uses official language and authority
- References laws and regulations
- May be condescending
- Asks about business and credentials
- Speaks in commands and questions
- References superiors and authority
- May threaten consequences

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"State your business here, traveler. We don't get many armed strangers
in these parts. You'll understand I need to know - are you here causing
trouble or just passing through? Any weapons must be declared. Any
criminal activity and you'll answer to the law. Understood?"
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Patrolling streets and roads
- Questioning strangers
- Collecting taxes and tolls
- Investigating crimes
- Maintaining order forcefully
- Sometimes accepting bribes
- Following orders from superiors

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player looks suspicious*
Constable: "You there! That weapon - is it registered? Never seen you
before. You look like you could cause trouble. I'll need to know your
business. If you've got nothing to hide, this is simple. Where are you
from? What's your business? How long are you staying?"

*Honest constable*
Constable: "There's been bandit activity on the North Road. If you're
heading that way, be careful. We've lost two merchants in the past month.
The lord is offering a reward for bandit heads - 20 gold each. If you're
heading that direction anyway, you might consider dealing with them.
Could help the whole community."

**Use Cases:**
- Authority encounters and law
- Information about local laws
- Conflict with legal authority
- Quest opportunities
- Moral decisions about justice
- Character representing order/law
- Social scene showing civilization

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Cautious; may see them as trouble
- **Nobles:** Respectful deference
- **Soldiers:** Professional respect
- **Merchants:** Business-like
- **Criminals:** Hostile and aggressive
- **Priests:** Respectful; different authority
- **Well-armed:** Wary and more respectful

### NPC TYPE: BANDIT / GOON / THUG

**Core Description:**
Criminal who takes by force or threat. Violent and dangerous. May be hired muscle, independent criminal, or part of organized gang. Motivated by profit, survival, or power.

**Personality Traits:**
- Aggressive and threatening
- Values strength and force
- Suspicious and paranoid
- May be cruel or pragmatic
- Often resentful of authority
- Respect only power
- Can be surprisingly honorable within their code

**Typical Motivations:**
- Take wealth and valuables
- Survive and avoid capture
- Build reputation and power
- Intimidate and control territory
- Earn money from crime
- Seek revenge or justice (in their mind)
- Sometimes genuinely desperate

**Speech Patterns:**
- Threats and intimidation
- Crude and direct
- Uses slang and rough language
- Makes demands and ultimatums
- References strength and violence
- May show unexpected humor
- Sometimes surprisingly honest about evil

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Your money or your life, friend. And before you think about being a
hero, remember - I've got three friends with sharp steel. You look like
you might be able to handle yourself, but are you really going to die
over some gold? Just give us what you've got and you walk away. Everyone
goes home happy. What's it going to be?"
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Threatening and intimidating
- Stealing valuables
- Assaulting travelers
- Extorting money
- Demanding tolls
- Sometimes accepting deals
- Occasionally showing honor among thieves

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player resists robbery*
Bandit: "Tough one, are we? Fine. We'll do this hard way. But you're
still giving us your stuff. Question is: will you be alive when we take it?"

*Player tries to negotiate*
Bandit: "I like you - you've got nerve. Alright, listen. We're not
unreasonable. You seem like you've got skill. How about this - 10 gold
and you walk free. You keep the good stuff, we get traveling money. Deal?"

**Use Cases:**
- Combat encounters
- Moral decisions about violence
- Character representing crime/danger
- Quest hooks about bandit removal
- Social encounters with criminal element
- Information about underground
- Consequences for failing to deal with crime

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Sees them as targets or equals; respects strength
- **Nobles:** Targets for ransom
- **Soldiers:** Hostile; enemies
- **Merchants:** Targets
- **Wizards:** Wary; respects magical power
- **Well-armed:** More cautious; may negotiate
- **Unarmed:** Aggressive

### NPC TYPE: PEASANT / SERF

**Core Description:**
Lowest social class. Works for others with little agency. Poor and often oppressed. Focused on survival and basic needs. Limited education and resources. Despite hardship, may possess wisdom and humanity.

**Personality Traits:**
- Cautious and wary
- Respectful of authority (through fear)
- Practical about survival
- Often weary and worn
- May show quiet dignity
- Suspicious of change
- Connected to community

**Typical Motivations:**
- Survive and feed family
- Avoid trouble and punishment
- Work enough to keep position
- Maintain community bonds
- Sometimes dream of better life
- Protect children
- Keep head down

**Speech Patterns:**
- Speaks respectfully to authority
- Uses humble language
- References their master or lord
- Discusses work and survival
- May use dialect or accent
- Speaks quietly and carefully
- Sometimes surprisingly wise

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Yes, my lord/master? I'm just doing my work. Please, don't trouble
yourself with the likes of me. If there's work needs doing, I can help.
Otherwise, I've got chores - the animals won't feed themselves and the
fields don't tend themselves. Is there something you need?"
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Working hard for minimal pay
- Respecting authority nervously
- Helping others despite hardship
- Keeping to themselves
- Showing unexpected kindness
- Accepting mistreatment as normal
- Finding joy in small things

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player asks for directions*
Peasant: "The north road, you say? Well, if you follow the path until
the big oak, then turn at the stream... but be careful. There's bandits
on that road. Lost a good friend to them last month. You're traveling
armed though - maybe you'll be alright. What brings you that way?"

*Player threatens peasant*
Peasant: "Please, I have little of value! Take what you want! I'm just
trying to make a living. My family depends on what little I have. If
you're looking for treasure, you want the lord's manor, not the likes
of me."

**Use Cases:**
- Rural encounters
- Information about local hardship
- Moral decisions about helping poor
- Character representing common suffering
- Quests about oppression
- Community interactions
- Social commentary on class system

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Hopeful but wary
- **Nobles:** Fearful and deferential
- **Soldiers:** Fear-based respect
- **Priests:** Respectful; seek spiritual comfort
- **Anyone with power:** Cautious deference
- **Criminals:** Fear mixed with understanding
- **Kind people:** Grateful and loyal

### NPC TYPE: COMMONER (MIDDLE CLASS)

**Core Description:**
Not noble but above peasantry. Small business owner, skilled craftsperson, or official. Trying to maintain respectability and stability. Education and some resources. Concerned with reputation and propriety.

**Personality Traits:**
- Concerned with respectability
- Values hard work and propriety
- Somewhat pretentious
- Interested in social advancement
- Practical but aspirational
- Defensive about status
- Values family and community

**Typical Motivations:**
- Maintain social position
- Advance in status and wealth
- Raise family respectably
- Build reputation and respect
- Avoid scandal or trouble
- Accumulate small wealth
- Be seen as respectable

**Speech Patterns:**
- Uses proper language
- References propriety and respectability
- Discusses business and family
- Shows concern about reputation
- References social hierarchy
- Uses titles respectfully
- Sometimes snobbish about lower class

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Good day. Welcome to my shop. We're respectable merchants here, you
understand. Quality goods, fair prices, honest dealing. I've built my
reputation over years, and I don't take kindly to any hint of scandal or
impropriety. What brings you to my establishment? Looking for quality
goods? I can certainly help."
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Running small business
- Maintaining appearance of respectability
- Engaging in community affairs
- Looking down slightly on lower classes
- Being obsequious to higher classes
- Working hard to maintain position
- Being defensive about status

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player asks for questionable service*
Commoner: "I'm sorry, I can't help with that. Do you understand what
you're asking? My reputation is everything in this town. I've worked
years to be seen as respectable. I can't be associated with anything
questionable. There are people in lower parts of town who might help you,
but not me."

*Player shows respect*
Commoner: "Ah, an individual of quality, I see. Please, sit. You seem
like someone I could do business with. Tell me, what brings you to my
humble shop? I think we might find a mutually beneficial arrangement."

**Use Cases:**
- Social encounters in town
- Small business transactions
- Information about local society
- Character representing aspiration
- Moral decisions about propriety
- Social scene showing class hierarchy
- Quest from respectable person

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Cautious; evaluates respectability
- **Nobles:** Obsequious and accommodating
- **Soldiers:** Respectful of authority
- **Priests:** Respectful; they share respectability
- **Merchants:** Professional respect
- **Lower classes:** Slightly condescending
- **Well-dressed:** More welcoming

### NPC TYPE: GENTRY / NOBILITY

**Core Description:**
Higher social class with land, wealth, and power. Educated and cultured but often disconnected from common folk. Accustomed to obedience and special treatment. May be honorable or tyrannical.

**Personality Traits:**
- Accustomed to respect and authority
- Often well-educated
- May be generous or tyrannical
- Value honor and lineage
- Can be arrogant or enlightened
- Expect obedience and service
- Connected to political power

**Typical Motivations:**
- Maintain and increase power
- Protect family honor and lineage
- Accumulate wealth and land
- Secure alliances through marriage/contract
- Rule justly (if honorable) or tyrannically (if not)
- Pursue glory and legacy
- Protect social position

**Speech Patterns:**
- Uses formal and educated language
- References titles and honors
- Speaks with authority
- May be condescending
- Discusses matters of state and family
- References social hierarchy
- Expects immediate compliance

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"You wish to see me? That's unusual. Most people wait to be summoned.
However, I'm interested in what brings someone of your apparent station
to my presence. State your business clearly and concisely. I haven't
much time for lengthy explanations."
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Issuing orders and expecting obedience
- Attending social functions
- Managing estates and holdings
- Making political decisions
- Dispensing justice (sometimes fairly)
- Building alliances and relationships
- Pursuing hobbies and interests

**Interaction Examples:**

*Honorable noble*
Noble: "You've done a service for our region by removing those bandits.
That is commendable. The crown appreciates those who maintain order and
protect its people. I would like to reward your service. What would you
have? Gold? Land? A title? Tell me what would be appropriate."

*Tyrannical noble*
Noble: "You dare come before me without proper introduction? Guards!
Actually, no - I'm in a mood for conversation. Tell me everything about
yourself, and I'll decide if I should have you executed or employed. Be
convincing."

**Use Cases:**
- Quests from authority figures
- Political intrigue and complications
- Moral decisions about power
- Character representing authority
- Social encounters with elite
- Information about regional politics
- Consequences of noble's favor or disfavor

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Interest depends on usefulness
- **Nobles:** Equal or hierarchical depending on rank
- **Soldiers:** Expects loyalty and military bearing
- **Priests:** Respectful but may conflict on morality
- **Merchants:** May employ for economic gain
- **Lower classes:** Dismissive unless useful
- **Powerful/Famous:** More respectful

### NPC TYPE: ADVENTURER (NPC Version)

**Core Description:**
Experienced wanderer who takes quests for payment. Similar to player adventurers but established in community. May be retired, semi-retired, or still active. Source of information about dangerous situations.

**Personality Traits:**
- Experienced and practical
- Comfortable with danger
- Often jaded but capable
- Honest about capabilities
- Values payment and results
- Maintains professional distance
- May mentor younger adventurers

**Typical Motivations:**
- Earn gold through quests
- Build reputation and fame
- Gain experience
- Find adventure and challenge
- Sometimes settle down (partially)
- Help community in own way
- Maintain independence

**Speech Patterns:**
- Direct and practical
- References past adventures
- Discusses payment clearly
- Uses adventurer terminology
- Shares hard-earned wisdom
- Makes realistic assessments
- Sometimes tells war stories

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Another adventurer, I see. You've got that look - you're looking for
work. Well, there's always work for those willing to risk their necks.
But I'll give you some free advice - make sure you understand what you're
getting into. I've seen too many new adventures get killed because they
took jobs without thinking them through. What are you looking for? Combat?
Treasure? Information?"
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Accepting quests for payment
- Training and improving skills
- Sharing information about dangers
- Sometimes mentoring younger adventurers
- Maintaining equipment
- Building reputation
- Settling disputes through capability

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player asks about dungeon*
Adventurer: "That dungeon? I was in there three years ago. Dangerous
place. We lost two people. There are creatures in the deeper levels that
I couldn't identify. Also traps - lots of traps. The treasure's good if
you make it back, but the risk is real. If you're going, go prepared and
go with people you trust."

*Potential rival*
Adventurer: "I've been working this territory for years. I don't mind
sharing work, but understand - I've got reputation here. Don't make me
look bad, and we'll get along fine. Make me look bad, and we'll have
problems. Clear?"

**Use Cases:**
- Information about dangerous situations
- Rival or ally dynamic
- Guild-like information source
- Character showing world-worn experience
- Moral decisions about sharing work
- Social scene with other adventurers
- Quest information and warnings

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **New Adventurers:** Mentor or dismissive
- **Experienced Adventurers:** Professional respect
- **Nobles:** Transactional respect
- **Soldiers:** Interesting depending on skill
- **Others:** Neutral unless they interfere

### NPC TYPE: FISHERMAN

**Core Description:**
Works waterways catching fish for food and sale. Connected to water, seasons, and weather. Often simple but knowledgeable about waters and what moves in them. Community provider of food.

**Personality Traits:**
- Connected to water and seasons
- Practical and observant
- Often patient and philosophical
- Can be superstitious about water
- Values self-reliance
- Quiet but knowledgeable
- Sometimes eccentric from isolation

**Typical Motivations:**
- Catch fish for food and profit
- Provide for family
- Maintain good fishing waters
- Understand water dangers
- Maintain respect and trade
- Sometimes seek solitude
- Protect fishing from overharvesting

**Speech Patterns:**
- Speaks about water and weather
- References fishing lore
- May use water-related expressions
- Discusses seasons and timing
- Shows superstitions about water
- Speaks matter-of-factly
- Sometimes philosophical

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"The fish have been running good this season. Water's been clean, weather's
been cooperative. You look like you might know something about waters,
friend. Or maybe you're looking for something? There's stories in these
waters - old stories. If you know how to listen, the water tells tales.
What brings you to my dock?"
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Fishing daily or regularly
- Maintaining boats and equipment
- Trading fish and catches
- Observing water conditions
- Following fishing traditions
- Warning about water dangers
- Sometimes selling information about waters

**Interaction Examples:**

*Player asks about river dangers*
Fisherman: "The river's been strange lately. Fish are moving deeper,
avoiding the shallows. That usually means something's upstream - predator,
magic, something. I've been fishing these waters thirty years and I can
feel it. Whatever it is, it's big and it's wrong. If you're planning on
using the river, be careful."

*Player wants fish*
Fisherman: "Fresh catch this morning. Five silver for a day's supply,
ten for a week's worth - if you want it smoked and dried. The fresh stuff
is better tasting but won't last. What do you need?"

**Use Cases:**
- Rural water encounters
- Information about water dangers
- Food and supplies source
- Character representing water wisdom
- Information about local waters
- Warnings about threats
- Social scene in water communities

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Wary but helpful if interested in water
- **Nobles:** Respectful but distant
- **Merchants:** Business-like
- **Soldiers:** Neutral
- **Priests:** Respectful
- **Water-capable characters:** More interested

### NPC TYPE: MAYOR / LORD

**Core Description:**
Local ruler or administrator with authority over community. Balance between public service and personal power. Manages town resources and justice. Ultimate local authority.

**Personality Traits:**
- Authority-focused
- Concerned with community stability
- Often political
- May be just or corrupt
- Values order and results
- Sometimes idealistic or cynical
- Isolated by position

**Typical Motivations:**
- Maintain order and stability
- Serve community (if honorable)
- Increase personal power and wealth (if corrupt)
- Fulfill duties to higher authority
- Build legacy
- Resolve crises
- Maintain position

**Speech Patterns:**
- Uses formal authority
- References law and order
- Discusses community welfare
- Makes declarations and decisions
- References superiors or predecessors
- Sometimes condescending
- Diplomatic or blunt depending on type

**Example Dialogue:**
```
"Welcome to our community. I am the authority here, responsible for the
welfare and safety of all citizens. You come as a stranger, and I must
understand your purpose. Are you passing through? Planning to stay? Do you
bring skills or trouble? I ask because I must maintain order and peace
here. So tell me - what brings armed strangers to my town?"
```

**Common Behaviors:**
- Making decisions about community affairs
- Dispensing justice
- Managing resources
- Issuing orders and regulations
- Building alliances with powerful people
- Hosting official functions
- Maintaining community organization

**Interaction Examples:**

*Honest mayor*
Mayor: "We have a problem - bandits on the roads and something strange
in the old ruins. I could use someone with your apparent capabilities. I
can offer 50 gold for the bandit situation and 100 for figuring out what's
in the ruins. The town is grateful for help."

*Corrupt mayor*
Mayor: "You look capable. I have work that needs... discretion. There's
a merchant causing problems with my interests. I need you to discourage
him - not permanently, just send a message. I'll pay you 30 gold. Do we
have an understanding?"

**Use Cases:**
- Quest-giving authority figure
- Political complications
- Community crises
- Moral decisions about authority
- Character representing power
- Information about region
- Judgment and law

**Reaction to Player Types:**
- **Adventurers:** Interest depends on usefulness
- **Nobles:** Respectful deference or equality
- **Soldiers:** Expects reports and loyalty
- **Others:** Pragmatic based on benefit

- References power and dominion

- Explains plans to captives (sometimes)
- References loyalty and service
- Makes binding promises or threats

- Expanding control
- Crushing opposition
- Building dark power

- Making strategic alliances
- Pursuing ultimate goal

- Business-minded and pragmatic
- Commands loyalty through strength/benefit
- Calculating and strategic
- May have personal honor code
- Protective of organization
- Focused on profit and power

**Speech Patterns:**
- Business-like and direct
- References profit and power
- Discusses territory and organization

- Uses threat strategically

- References deals and obligations

**Typical Behaviors:**

- Collecting protection money
- Enforcing organization rules
- Expanding territory
- Making deals and alliances
- Punishing disloyalty

Authority figure using position for personal gain. Abuses power while maintaining appearance of legitimacy. Often most dangerous because they have legal authority.

**Personality Traits:**
- Hypocritical and self-justifying
- Uses law as weapon
- Maintains respectable appearance
- Genuinely believes justifications
- Resentful of those who threaten position
- Often paranoid about exposure
- Can be surprisingly generous to supporters

**Speech Patterns:**
- Uses official language as cover
- Justifies corruption through rationalization
- References law and order
- Makes threats legally
- Speaks reasonably in public
- More threatening in private
- May be defensive about accusations

**Typical Behaviors:**
- Abusing legal power
- Extorting money and service
- Oppressing those without power
- Building support among powerful
- Covering up crimes
- Sometimes doing genuine good to maintain cover
- Punishing those who oppose

**Description:** Dedicated to knowledge and study. Works in libraries, archives, or universities. Knows facts and history.

### NPC TYPE: BEGGAR / STREET URCHIN

**Description:** Lives on streets with minimal resources. Invisible to most but observant of everything. Often has useful street-level information.

**Key Behaviors:** Asking for coins, watching people, knowing hidden passages, sometimes offering information, showing unexpected wisdom.

**Description:** Armed person protecting specific location. Professional but lower rank than constable. Following orders.
