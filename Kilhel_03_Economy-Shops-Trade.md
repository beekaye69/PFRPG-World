**Vendor Locations:** 50+ shops across 4 neighborhoods

## 1. KILHEL ECONOMIC OVERVIEW

### Economic Profile
**Type:** Mixed feudal-merchant economy  
**Primary Drivers:**
- Trade (goods flowing through city)
- Craft production (quality artisanal goods)
- Agriculture (hinterland farming)
- Services (hospitality, professional)

**Currency Usage:**
- Gold pieces (gp): Primary for large trades
- Silver pieces (sp): Common for daily commerce
- Copper pieces (cp): Minor transactions
- Exchange rate: 1 gp = 10 sp = 100 cp

**Economic Health:** Strong
- Merchant activity: Thriving
- Employment: Generally available
- Prices: Stable (slight seasonal variance)
- Growth: Modest expansion year-over-year

### Neighborhood Economic Tiers
**Downtown:** Expensive (+20-30% markup)
- Premium location
- Tourist/wealthy customer base
- High overhead

**Richman:** Very Expensive (+40-60% markup)
- Exclusive clientele
- Quality focus
- Luxury tier pricing

**East Side:** Standard (baseline prices)
- Working class economy
- Value-focused
- Competitive pricing

**Lower Kilhel:** Standard (baseline prices)
- Middle class market
- Reasonable pricing
- Balanced quality/cost

## 7. ECONOMIC CYCLES & SEASONAL IMPACTS

### Spring (Market Awakening)
- Market expansion (new vendors)
- Price increase (+5-10%)
- Tourism begins
- New product availability
- Construction/labor demands increase

### Summer (Peak Trading)
- Market overflowing (peak season)
- Prices stable to high
- Highest tourist season
- Agricultural goods abundant
- Maximum economic activity

### Autumn (Harvest Season)
- Agricultural focus (preservation goods)
- Prices peak (+10-15%)
- Final trading season
- Preparation for winter
- Reduced tourism

### Winter (Scarcity)
- Market reduces (limited goods)
- Prices highest (+15-25%)
- Preserved goods only
- Minimal tourism
- Economic slowdown
