## 2. TERRAIN & ENVIRONMENTAL FEATURES

### Landscape Characteristics

**Primary Terrain Type:** Valley Floor (protected)
- Elevation: 1,200 feet above sea level
- Slope: Gentle (< 3% grade)
- Soil Quality: EXCELLENT (alluvial deposits, dark loam)
- Drainage: Good (natural slope to creek)
- Frost Line: Moderate (4-5 months winter, year-round springs)

**Surrounding Features:**
- North: Deciduous/mixed forest (healthy, 60+ year old trees)
- East: Agricultural fields transitioning to forest at 0.5 miles
- South: Crystal Creek (cold, fast water, 15 feet wide, 3 feet deep)
- West: Forest with natural trail to Crossroads

**Water Access:**
- Primary: Crystal Creek (fresh, clean, cold)
- Secondary: 2 hand-dug wells (community maintained)
- Tertiary: Spring (northeast corner, seasonal flow)
- Quality: EXCELLENT (tested by Marta Farmwright, herbalist)

**Forest Resources:**
- Timber: Mixed hardwood (oak, ash, beech), sustainable harvest
- Undergrowth: Edible mushrooms, forage, medicinal herbs
- Game: Deer, rabbits, birds (not overhunted)
- Hazards: Occasional wolf packs (spring/winter), rare goblin scouts (recent)

**Climate Pattern:**
- Spring: Moderate rain, muddy (March-May), planting season
- Summer: Warm, dry (June-August), growing season
- Fall: Cool, wet (September-November), harvest
- Winter: Cold, snow (December-February), frozen ground
- Growing Season: 140-150 days (good for hardy crops, grains, root vegetables)

```

  - Outhouses: 2 (one north, one south of settlement)

  - Maintenance: Monthly pit pump-out to compost area

  - Rotation: 6-month cycles (4 piles, rotating)

  - Stables manure: Collected daily, moved to pile
  - Livestock pasture: Left in-situ (natural fertilization)
  - Processing: Mixture with vegetable waste (composting)
  - Destination: Fields (plowed under in rotation)

  - Laundry/washing water: Runs to designated soak area
  - Bathwater: Limited (communal bath day, summer)
  - Direction: Downslope from wells, toward creek

Kitchen Waste:
  - Food scraps: Composted (mixed with animal scraps)
  - Bones/fat: Collected for rendering (soap, fuel)
  - Garden waste: Piled separately (leaf litter)
```
