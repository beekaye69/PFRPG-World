## üìñ CORE MAGIC MECHANICS (From PFRPG Rulebook)

### Combat Magic vs Free-Form Magic

**Combat Magic:**
- Location: Used during combat encounters
- Limit: 2 spells per encounter (total)
- Action: Casting takes a combat action
- Types: Can cast same spell twice OR two different spells
- Expansion: Heroic Reroll allows +1 additional spell per encounter
- Range: Per spell description
- Examples: Dark Bolt, Rain of Fire, Confusion, Summon Monster, Arcane Armor, Aid from Beyond

**Free-Form Magic:**
- Location: Used outside of combat only
- Limit: 2 spells per session (total)
- Action: Casting uses task resolution system
- Types: Any creative magic (food creation, levitation, etc.)
- Expansion: Heroic Reroll allows +1 additional spell per session
- Duration: Conjured items last 24 hours
- Examples: Conjure food, create boat, create rope, levitate, speak with animals, detect secret doors

### Key Difference: Predictability

**Combat Spells:** Defined mechanics, known effects, roll-based execution

**Free-Form Spells:** Creative within boundaries, PM discretion on success/failure, based on task difficulty

### SPELL #1: DARK BOLT

**Official Mechanics (From Rulebook):**
- Type: Ranged Attack Action
- Damage: CS d6
- Reroll: Roll twice, take best result
- Special: May explode (if natural 6, roll again and add)
- Target: Single enemy at range
- Action Cost: 1 combat action

**What Dark Bolt Is:**
Single-target burst damage spell with exploding potential. Best against one strong enemy or when you need guaranteed damage.

**Strengths:**
- ‚úÖ Guaranteed single-target damage
- ‚úÖ Explosion potential for massive damage
- ‚úÖ Reroll means better average damage
- ‚úÖ Works at any range (weapon range)
- ‚úÖ Can solve "tough single enemy" encounters

**Weaknesses:**
- ‚ùå Only hits one target
- ‚ùå Useless against multiple enemies
- ‚ùå Takes a spell slot (2 max per encounter)
- ‚ùå Can fail if rolls low (d6 range: 1-6)
- ‚ùå Target gets Defence roll after

**When to Use Dark Bolt:**
- ‚úì Single strong enemy (Troll, Ogre, Boss)
- ‚úì Need focused damage on key target
- ‚úì Multiple weak enemies (one per turn strategy)
- ‚úì When Rain of Fire won't work (enemies spread out)

**When NOT to Use Dark Bolt:**
- ‚úó Multiple enemies close together (use Rain of Fire instead)
- ‚úó Crowd control needed (use Confusion instead)
- ‚úó Need ally summon (use Summon Monster instead)
- ‚úó Want to prevent attack (use Arcane Armor instead)

#### DARK BOLT EXAMPLE 1: Single Strong Enemy

**Setup:**
- Wizard at 4/4 HP (full health)
- Enemy: Ogre (CS d6, 12 HP)
- Party: Just Wizard and one Fighter
- Fighter is fighting Ogre already, Ogre is winning

**Situation:**
Wizard needs to contribute damage. Fighter is taking 4+ damage per round. Wizard has 2 spell slots this encounter.

**Decision:**
Cast Dark Bolt on Ogre. (Saving second spell for possible emergency healing from items or future turns.)

**Execution:**
- Wizard: "I cast Dark Bolt at the Ogre!"

- "I also rolled, so you reroll. Second roll = 5. Take the best: 5 damage."
- Ogre Defence: Rolls d6 = 3
- Ogre takes: 5 - 3 = 2 damage
- Ogre HP: 12 - 2 = 10/12

**Analysis:**
Good hit (5 damage before Defence). Ogre damaged but still strong. Wizard contributed meaningful damage.

**Outcome:** Fighter continues melee while Ogre is weakened. Combat continues next round.

### SPELL #2: RAIN OF FIRE

**Official Mechanics (From Rulebook):**
- Type: Area Ranged Attack Action
- Targets: Up to 4 targets in close proximity (PM discretion)
- Damage per target: CS d6-1
- Reroll: Roll twice, take best result
- Action Cost: 1 combat action
- Special: Exceptional for group encounters

**What Rain of Fire Is:**
Area-of-effect damage spell. Hits multiple enemies at once with same damage roll. Best against groups of weaker enemies.

**Strengths:**
- ‚úÖ Hits up to 4 enemies with ONE spell
- ‚úÖ Single damage roll hits all (efficient)
- ‚úÖ Reroll means consistent damage
- ‚úÖ Best crowd control via damage
- ‚úÖ Often kills weak enemies instantly

**Weaknesses:**
- ‚ùå Enemies must be close together
- ‚ùå Slightly lower damage (d6-1 vs d6)
- ‚ùå Takes a spell slot
- ‚ùå Each target gets Defence roll
- ‚ùå Doesn't control strong enemies

**When to Use Rain of Fire:**
- ‚úì Multiple enemies close together (Goblin pack, etc.)
- ‚úì Want to eliminate many weak enemies at once
- ‚úì Need to control positioning (area denial)
- ‚úì Save spell slots (1 spell = 4 targets)
- ‚úì Softening up groups before melee

**When NOT to Use Rain of Fire:**
- ‚úó Single strong enemy (waste, use Dark Bolt)
- ‚úó Enemies spread far apart (can't hit all)
- ‚úó Need to prevent attacks (use Confusion or Arcane Armor)
- ‚úó Need ally summon (use Summon Monster)

### SPELL #3: CONFUSION

**Official Mechanics (From Rulebook):**
- Type: Crowd Control
- Effect: Enemies can't move or make Attack Actions (3 rounds)
- Still can use Defence Actions (still block damage)
- Success Condition: Each affected enemy makes tough task roll
- Failure: Affected enemy stays confused for duration
- Action Cost: 1 combat action
- Reroll: Spell duration rolled as d6-3 (minimum 1 round)

**What Confusion Is:**
Powerful crowd control spell. Stops enemies from attacking or moving for duration. Best against dangerous enemy groups.

**Strengths:**
- ‚úÖ Stops ALL enemies from attacking (if they fail roll)
- ‚úÖ Prevents movement entirely
- ‚úÖ Lets party control next rounds
- ‚úÖ Stacks with damage spells
- ‚úÖ Excellent for saving party from wipeout

**Weaknesses:**
- ‚ùå Enemies can still Defence (block damage)
- ‚ùå Enemies get tough task roll (often succeed)
- ‚ùå Only works 3 rounds max (usually)
- ‚ùå Undead immune (important limitation)
- ‚ùå Takes a spell slot

**When to Use Confusion:**
- ‚úì Party is losing (prevent damage while regrouping)
- ‚úì Enemy group is dangerous (stop attacks)
- ‚úì Need time (3 rounds to reposition/heal)
- ‚úì Before casting other spells (set up Rain of Fire)
- ‚úì Against non-undead groups

**When NOT to Use Confusion:**
- ‚úó Undead enemies (they're immune!)
- ‚úó Single enemy (waste of spell)
- ‚úó Can kill enemies instead (use damage spells)
- ‚úó Enemies already losing (overkill)

### SPELL #4: SUMMON MONSTER

**Official Mechanics (From Rulebook):**
- Type: Summons an ally under caster's control
- Duration: Roll d6-2 (minimum 2 rounds total)
- Control: Doesn't take up caster's action (PM discretion)
- Movement: Summon appears adjacent to caster
- Can Attack: Summons can attack on caster's initiative
- Actions: Follows caster's orders
- Monster Type: Random d6 roll determines type

**Monster Summoning Table:**
```
Roll 1-4: Choose Goblin OR Giant Centipede
Roll 5-6: Add Orc, Skeleton, Wolf, or Giant Spider to choices

If roll 6, re-roll:
  On another 6: Can add Minotaur, Ogre, or Troll to choices
```

**Summoned Monsters Stats:**
- Goblin: Weak (CS d6-3, ~3 HP)
- Giant Centipede: Weak (CS d6-3, ~4 HP)
- Orc: Average (CS d6-2, ~5 HP)
- Skeleton: Average (CS d6-2, ~4 HP) [Undead]
- Wolf: Average (CS d6-2, ~4 HP)
- Giant Spider: Average (CS d6-2, ~5 HP)
- Minotaur: Tough (CS d6-1, ~8 HP) [Rare]
- Ogre: Tough (CS d6-1, ~10 HP) [Rare]
- Troll: Powerful (CS d6, ~12 HP) [Very Rare, with Regenerate]

**What Summon Monster Is:**
Creates a temporary ally that fights for the caster. Scales from weak Goblins to powerful Trolls. Best for extending party numbers and tanking.

**Strengths:**
- ‚úÖ Creates ally to fight with you
- ‚úÖ Monsters can take hits meant for you
- ‚úÖ Adds DPS to party
- ‚úÖ Can summon strong monsters (Ogre, Minotaur)
- ‚úÖ Lasts 2-6 rounds (decent duration)

**Weaknesses:**
- ‚ùå Duration is random (d6-2)
- ‚ùå Monster type is random (might get weak Goblin)
- ‚ùå Takes a spell slot
- ‚ùå Monster follows caster's will (might not prioritize optimally)
- ‚ùå Summoned monster has limited HP

**When to Use Summon Monster:**
- ‚úì Party is outnumbered (add extra combatant)
- ‚úì Need a tank (monster absorbs damage)
- ‚úì Want flexible control (order monster around)
- ‚úì Have bad RNG with other spells
- ‚úì Single strong enemy fight (summon is backup)

**When NOT to Use Summon Monster:**
- ‚úó Already winning combat (waste)
- ‚úó Need immediate damage (damage spells better)
- ‚úó Summoned monster is too weak (unlucky roll)
- ‚úó Group is already optimal (no room for ally)

### SPELL #5: ARCANE ARMOR

**Official Mechanics (From Rulebook):**
- Type: Buff/Defense spell
- Effect: Target makes all Defence Actions at CS d6 (for duration)
- Reroll: Roll twice and take the best result
- Duration: Entire encounter
- Target: One character (self or ally)
- Action Cost: 1 combat action

**What Arcane Armor Is:**
Enhances Defence rolls for one character. Makes them much harder to hit. Best when a key ally is under heavy attack.

**Strengths:**
- ‚úÖ Boosts Defence significantly (everyone gets CS d6)
- ‚úÖ Works entire encounter (one-time cast)
- ‚úÖ Can be cast on Fighter (tank) or vulnerable ally
- ‚úÖ Reroll means consistent Defence
- ‚úÖ Stacks with actual armor

**Weaknesses:**
- ‚ùå Only affects one person
- ‚ùå Takes a spell slot
- ‚ùå Doesn't prevent damage (just reduces it)
- ‚ùå Requires choosing good target
- ‚ùå Limited benefit if target already good at Defence

**When to Use Arcane Armor:**
- ‚úì Fighter is taking heavy damage (boost tanking)
- ‚úì Squishy ally needs protection (Cleric, Wizard)
- ‚úì Want to ensure ally survival
- ‚úì Party has one key person (protect that person)
- ‚úì Encounter will last many rounds (long benefit)

**When NOT to Use Arcane Armor:**
- ‚úó Already winning easily (overkill)
- ‚úó Need damage instead (use Dark Bolt)
- ‚úó Need crowd control (use Confusion)
- ‚úó Need healing (other spells better)
- ‚úó Target already has good Defence

#### ARCANE ARMOR EXAMPLE 1: Fighter Protection

**Setup:**
- Fighter is getting pounded (2/10 HP)
- Enemy is strong (CS d6, consistent 3-4 damage)
- Wizard hasn't used spells yet

**Impact:**
- Fighter Defence rolls went from d6 (average 3.5) to d6 (same)
- But wait, Fighter's CS is d6 normally! What's the difference?
- Difference: Fighter already rolls d6. But the reroll mechanic means TWO d6 rolls taking best.
- New: Fighter rolls d6, then rolls d6 again, takes best = average 4.5 instead of 3.5

**Analysis:**
Average Defence increased from 3.5 to 4.5 (roughly +1). Against 3-4 damage attacks, this might fully block one hit per encounter.

**Combat:**
- Next round enemy attacks
- Fighter Defence (new): Rolls 4, re-rolls, gets 5, takes 5
- Enemy damage: 4
- Fighter blocked: 5 (blocks all!)
- Fighter survives

**Outcome:** Arcane Armor enabled Fighter survival. Second spell still available if needed.

### SPELL #6: AID FROM BEYOND

**Official Mechanics (From Rulebook):**
- Type: Party support/buff spell
- Effect: Other characters in party receive Heroic Reroll
- Duration: For use only during current encounter
- Action Cost: 1 combat action
- Special: Can only be used once per encounter normally
- Reroll: One additional use if Wizard uses Heroic Reroll

**What Aid from Beyond Is:**
Grants temporary Heroic Rerolls to allies. Best when party needs to succeed on crucial rolls.

**Strengths:**
- ‚úÖ Helps entire party (multiple people can benefit)
- ‚úÖ Heroic Rerolls are valuable (let allies retry failed rolls)
- ‚úÖ Can swing tight encounters
- ‚úÖ Works with all party members
- ‚úÖ Party can use Rerolls for anything (attack, Defence, spells, tasks)

**Weaknesses:**
- ‚ùå Only gives reroll (doesn't guarantee success)
- ‚ùå Party might waste rerolls (use on non-critical)
- ‚ùå Takes a spell slot
- ‚ùå Allies must use rerolls optimally
- ‚ùå Requires good party communication

**When to Use Aid from Beyond:**
- ‚úì Party needs lucky rolls to win (give them chances)
- ‚úì Important attack/Defence is coming up (prepare)
- ‚úì Party doesn't have many Heroic Rerolls left
- ‚úì Multiple allies at critical HP (each might need reroll)
- ‚úì Single spell slot can help whole party

**When NOT to Use Aid from Beyond:**
- ‚úó Already winning (overkill)
- ‚úó Party has plenty of Heroic Rerolls (waste)
- ‚úó Need damage instead (damage spells more direct)
- ‚úó Allies won't use rerolls well (bad coordination)

## üìä SPELL RESOURCE MANAGEMENT

**Key Mechanic:** 2 Spells Per Encounter

You have exactly 2 spell slots per encounter. You must choose:
- Option A: Use both on same spell type (2x Dark Bolt)
- Option B: Split between different spells (Dark Bolt + Confusion)
- Option C: Use 1 spell, save 1 (hedge bet)

**Resource Management Examples:**

### Scenario 1: Known Strong Enemy (Troll)
```
Expected: Single boss fight
Strategy: Save both spells for single enemy
  Spell 1: Dark Bolt (opening damage)
  Spell 2: Dark Bolt (finish attempt)
Risk: If Troll has friends, second spell wastes
```

### Scenario 2: Unknown Encounter
```
Expected: Unknown enemy type
Strategy: Hedge with flexibility
  Spell 1: Summon Monster (adds combatant, see what's coming)
  Spell 2: Save for response (damage, control, or buff)
Risk: Summoned monster might be weak, wastes spell slot
```

### Scenario 3: Dangerous Situation
```
Expected: Party losing, enemies strong
Strategy: Control first, damage second
  Spell 1: Confusion (stop enemy attacks)
  Spell 2: Damage spell if party survives confusion
Risk: Confusion might fail (tough task rolls)
```

### Scenario 4: Optimization Play
```
Expected: Multiple weak enemies
Strategy: Maximize efficiency
  Spell 1: Rain of Fire (kill multiple)
  Spell 2: Save or use on stragglers
Risk: Enemies spread apart, Rain of Fire misses
```

## üåü FREE-FORM MAGIC (Out of Combat)

**What It Is:**
Creative magic cast outside of combat. Unlimited possibilities. Uses task resolution.

**Mechanics:**
- Location: Out of combat only
- Limit: 2 per session
- Action: Task roll based on complexity/duration/scope
- Success: PM determines if spell works
- Failure: Spell attempt is lost
- Duration: Usually 24 hours if conjured

**Classic Examples (From Rulebook):**
- Conjure food
- Create boat
- Create rope
- Create bridge (over lava)
- Levitate (to top of tower)
- Speak with animals
- Detect secret doors
- Send telepathic message
- Breathe underwater

**PM Adjudication:**
PM decides success/failure based on:
1. **Complexity:** Simple (laughable) vs Complex (very difficult)
2. **Duration:** How long does it last? (24 hours standard)
3. **Scope:** How big? How many people affected?
