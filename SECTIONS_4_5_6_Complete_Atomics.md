- Example 2: Dwarf Fighter using Underground Expertise to traverse trapped dungeon

- How Underground Expertise combines with dungeon navigation
- When Poison Resistance applies (PM discretion on poison types)

**Mini FAQ:**
- Q: Does Dark Vision work in magical darkness? A: PM discretion
- Q: How much advantage does Poison Resistance give? A: PM discretion on specific poison

- When to use Dark Vision (obvious)
- When Underground Expertise helps (navigation, room finding, trap detection)

**Synergies:** Works with any class; excellent with Cleric (healing + underground safety)

## SECTION 4.2: ELF COMPLETE GUIDE

**Overview:** Keen-eyed adventurer, best explorer, combat-secure

**Traits:**
- Dark Vision (60 ft in darkness)
- Secret Door Finding (Simple 3+ difficulty finding hidden doors/secrets)
- Paralysis Immunity (can't be paralyzed)
- Surprise Resistance (only surprised on roll of 1, vs. normal 1-3 range)

**Optimal Classes:** Ranger, Wizard

**Gameplay Examples:**
- Example 1: Elf finding secret door that party missed
- Example 2: Elf immune to paralysis monster ability (party vulnerability avoided)
- Example 3: Elf ambushed but only on natural 1 roll (massive survival advantage)

**PM Reasoning:**
- How Surprise Resistance interacts with ambush mechanics
- Paralysis Immunity vs. specific enemy abilities
- Secret Door Finding in dungeon design

**Mini FAQ:**
- Q: Can I use Secret Door Finding reroll on regular searches? A: PM discretion
- Q: Does Paralysis Immunity apply to all control effects? A: No, paralysis specifically
- Q: How often do I need to roll for Secret Doors? A: PM discretion on frequency

**Decision Matrix:**
- Exploration advantage: Secret Door Finding (guaranteed or near-guaranteed)
- Combat security: Surprise Resistance (safety from ambushes)
- Monster encounters: Paralysis Immunity (avoid debilitating effects)

**Optimal Play:** Primary explorer, front-line perception advantage, combat security

**Synergies:** Best with Ranger (exploration + ranged safety) or Wizard (exploration advantage)

## SECTION 4.3: HALFLING COMPLETE GUIDE

**Overview:** Stealthy expert, excellent hider, small-size advantage

**Traits:**
- Large Monster Defense (reroll vs. large enemies)
- Forest Hiding (Laughable 2+ difficulty hiding in forests)
- Hidden State (undetectable when hidden)

**Optimal Classes:** Thief

**Gameplay Examples:**
- Example 1: Halfling Thief uses Forest Hiding for guaranteed stealth
- Example 2: Halfling uses Large Monster Defense against Boss monster
- Example 3: Halfling combines Hidden State with Backstab for massive damage

**PM Reasoning:**
- How Hidden State combines with Backstab (double damage guaranteed)
- When Large Monster Defense applies (size-based creatures)
- Forest Hiding advantage in combat positioning

**Mini FAQ:**
- Q: Can I use Forest Hiding in dungeons? A: No, forests specifically
- Q: Does Hidden State work mid-combat? A: No, requires pre-combat positioning
- Q: Can I remain Hidden and attack multiple times? A: First attack breaks hidden state

**Decision Matrix:**
- Stealth approach: Forest Hiding (nearly guaranteed)
- Boss encounters: Large Monster Defense (reroll vs. tough enemies)
- Combat opening: Hidden State + Backstab (massive opening damage)

**Optimal Play:** Thief positioning specialist, stealth expert, boss fight advantage

**Synergies:** Excellent with Thief (combined stealth + backstab)

- Example 3: Human Cleric ensures party survives with resource advantage

**PM Reasoning:**
- Extra Heroic Reroll enables conservative play (more safety net)
- Resource management advantage throughout session
- Pairs with any class effectively

**Mini FAQ:**
- Q: Can I save unused Heroic Rerolls for next session? A: No, resets each session
- Q: Does Human bonus stack with Amulet of Legends? A: Yes (2 human + 2 amulet = 4 total max)

**Decision Matrix:**
- When to use first reroll (save for crucial moment)
- When second reroll becomes safety net (late session resource)

**Optimal Play:** Strategic resource management, reliable safety net, any class choice

**Synergies:** Works with all classes equally well

## SECTION 5.1: COMBAT MAGIC FRAMEWORK

**System Overview:**
- 2 spells per encounter (resets after rest)
- Any spell combination allowed
- Each spell costs 1 action in combat
- Spells use CS rolls (d6 modified by spell)

**Strategic Spell Selection by Encounter Type:**

| Encounter | Spell 1 | Spell 2 | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single Strong Enemy | Dark Bolt | Confusion | Damage + control |
| Multiple Weak Enemies | Rain of Fire | Dark Bolt | Area damage priority |
| Defensive Encounter | Arcane Armor | Confusion | Defense + control |
| Support Role | Aid from Beyond | Arcane Armor | Buff allies |
| Unknown | Dark Bolt | Confusion | Balanced baseline |

**Gameplay Examples:**
- Example 1: Wizard selects Dark Bolt + Rain of Fire for 4-enemy encounter
- Example 2: Wizard uses Aid from Beyond to grant party Heroic Reroll in boss fight
- Example 3: Wizard casts Confusion to immobilize dangerous enemy

**Strategic Use:**
- Exploration problem-solving

- Creative solutions

## SECTION 5.4: BARDIC MAGIC SYSTEM

**System Overview:**
- 7 different songs available
- Sing continuously for 3+ rounds
- Party-wide effect (radius-based)
- One song active at a time
- Can switch between songs (end one, start new, restart 3-round timer)

**The Seven Songs Detailed:**

### Song 1: Hymn of Mighty
- Effect: +1 damage all party attacks
- Best Use: Offensive encounters
- Duration: 3+ rounds
- Range: Party hearing range

### Song 2: Lay of Deflection
- Effect: +1 defense all party defense rolls
- Best Use: Defensive encounters
- Duration: 3+ rounds
- Range: Party hearing range

### Song 3: Ballad of Legends
- Effect: All party rolls of 3+ count as Heroic Reroll use
- Best Use: Long encounters (generate resources)
- Duration: 3+ rounds
- Range: Party hearing range

### Song 4: Song of Mending
- Effect: Heal 1 hp/round all party members
- Best Use: Sustain damaged party
- Duration: 3+ rounds (continuous healing)
- Range: Party hearing range

### Song 5: Chant of Renewal
- Effect: Full party healing (all restore full HP)
- Best Use: Emergency reset
- Duration: One-time use
- Range: Party hearing range

### Song 6: Melody of Madness
- Effect: Enemies in aura become confused/disoriented
- Best Use: Area control
- Duration: 3+ rounds
- Range: Aura radius (30 ft typical)

### Song 7: Shanty of Pain
- Effect: Party reflects damage taken (counter-attack)
- Best Use: Against multi-hitters
- Duration: 3+ rounds
- Range: Party hearing range

**Strategic Selection Guide:**

| Encounter Profile | Song Choice | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Weak enemies (easy win) | Hymn of Mighty | Amplify offense, end quick |
| Strong enemies (defensive) | Lay of Deflection | Reduce damage taken |
| Long fight expected | Ballad of Legends | Generate resource advantage |
| Party low on HP | Song of Mending | Sustain health |
| Party critical | Chant of Renewal | Full reset (one-time) |
| Area full of enemies | Melody of Madness | Control area |
| Enemy multi-hitter | Shanty of Pain | Reflection advantage |

## SECTION 5.5: MAGIC INTERACTIONS

**Magic + Combat System:**

**Spell in Combat:**
- Costs 1 action (same as attack)
- Counts toward 2 spells per encounter
- Resolves immediately
- Can trigger explosions (natural 6 on damage)

**Magic + Heroic Reroll:**
- Can use Heroic Reroll on spell rolls
- Recommended for crucial checks
- Strategic resource management

**Magic + Items:**
- Some items grant spell access
- Staffs hold multiple spell slots
- Wands focus single spell
- Jewelry buffs magic damage (rare)

**Magic + Party Synergies:**
- Bard songs buff Wizard spells (indirectly via party)
- Cleric healing enables Wizard aggression (reduced defense need)
- Wizard spells enable Fighter dominance (Confusion frees Fighter to attack)

## SECTION 6.5: CRAFTING SYSTEM

**Overview:**
- Blacksmith NPC (PM-provided)
- Runic Forge upgrades (x1 → x2 → x3)
- Arcane Gems as currency

**Crafting Process:**

1. Collect 3 Arcane Gems
2. Choose base weapon/armor
3. Visit Blacksmith
4. Craft item (immediate or time-based)
5. Receive x1 magical item

**Upgrade Process (Runic Forge):**

- x1 to x2: +3 additional Arcane Gems (6 total)
- x2 to x3: +3 additional Arcane Gems (9 total)

**Gem Collection Strategy:**

| Campaign Phase | Target | Gems Needed | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early | First x1 item | 3 gems | Collect quickly |
| Mid | Second x1 or upgrade to x2 | 6-9 gems | Plan next item |
| Late | Multiple x2 or x3 items | 15+ gems | Sustained crafting |

**Gameplay Examples:**
- Example 1: Party collects 3 gems, crafts Focus x1 weapon for Fighter
- Example 2: Party upgrades Focus x1 to Focus x2 after collecting 3 more gems
- Example 3: Party crafts multiple items (Deflect x1 + Ring + Focus x1) with 9+ gems
