**Section 2: Six Dungeon Design Patterns**

1. **LINEAR DUNGEON** - Single path progression (A‚ÜíB‚ÜíC‚ÜíD)
   - Best for: Small dungeons, tight story, time-limited play
   - Characteristics: Easy navigation, limited replayability
   - Example: Crypt

2. **BRANCHING DUNGEON** - Multiple paths from central hub
   - Best for: Medium dungeons, exploration choice
   - Characteristics: Player agency, varied difficulty options
   - Example: Multi-room structure with side branches

3. **CIRCULAR/LOOP DUNGEON** - Connected ring structure
   - Best for: Medium dungeons, boss-in-center design
   - Characteristics: Multiple approach angles, flanking opportunities
   - Example: Boss surrounded by connecting rooms

4. **GRID/MAZE DUNGEON** - Complex interconnected (3x3, 4x4 grids)
   - Best for: Large dungeons, exploration focus, getting lost
   - Characteristics: Many viable routes, discovered feeling
   - Example: 12-20 room interconnected network

5. **VERTICAL/TIERED DUNGEON** - Multiple levels with progression
   - Best for: Large dungeons, difficulty escalation
   - Characteristics: Level themes vary, difficulty increases downward
   - Example: Level 1 guards, Level 2 rituals, Level 3 boss

6. **HUB-AND-SPOKE DUNGEON** - Central connection with branches
   - Best for: Campaign dungeons, non-linear exploration
   - Characteristics: Branch completion order flexible, modular difficulty
   - Example: 20-40 room campaign structure
