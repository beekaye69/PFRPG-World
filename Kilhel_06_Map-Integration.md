## DOWNTOWN / MARKET SQUARE (Center - Yellow/Brown)

### Key Locations to Place:

**1. Central Market Plaza**
- Location: Heart of Downtown
- Size: ~200ft × 150ft open space
- Features: Fountain, announcement stone, vendor stalls
- Daily vendors: 40-60 rotating stalls
- Usage: Market center, public gathering

**2. Duke's Administrative Building**
- Location: Near center, formal placement
- Size: 2-story stone/wood structure
- Features: Official offices, guard presence, documentation
- Guards: 4-6 typically present
- Function: Government center

**3. Merchant's Guild Hall**
- Location: Downtown, prestigious position
- Size: 3-story wooden building
- Features: Guild offices, meeting rooms, trade records
- Key NPC: Master Geldan
- Function: Commercial authority

**4. Temple of the Main Faith**
- Location: Downtown, prominent position
- Size: Large religious building (capacity 100-150)
- Features: Impressive architecture, religious function
- Key NPC: Father Aldwin
- Function: Primary religious center

**5. The Silver Swan Inn**
- Location: Central Downtown, easy access
- Size: 3-story building (12-15 rooms)
- Features: Tavern, common room, stables
- Key NPC: Marta Goldleaf (proprietor)
- Function: Primary accommodation hub

**6. Valdor's Fine Dining**
- Location: Downtown, exclusive placement
- Size: Medium restaurant building
- Features: Fine dining, 20-30 seats
- Key NPC: Chef Valdor (elf)
- Function: Upscale dining

**7. Permanent Shops** (Place around plaza perimeter)
- Brax's Forge (weapons/armor)
- The Silk Merchant (fine goods)
- Dimitri's Gems (jewelry)
- Literary Scholar's Collection (books)
- The Fine Tailor (clothing)
- General Goods merchant
- Banking House

**Street Layout:**
- Main street: Runs through center (commerce flow)
- Secondary streets: Connect to other neighborhoods
- Plazas: Open gathering spaces

## EAST SIDE (East Green Zone)

### Key Locations to Place:

**1. The East Market**
- Location: Central to East Side
- Size: ~150ft × 100ft open space
- Features: Morning market, vendor stalls
- Vendors: 30-40 regular stalls
- Function: Working-class marketplace

**2. The Dwarven Quarter**
- Location: Northern East Side cluster
- Size: ~200ft × 150ft residential area
- Features: Dwarf homes, community gathering
- Population: 50-80 dwarves
- Function: Dwarven cultural center

**3. The Stone Temple**
- Location: Dwarven Quarter or central East Side
- Size: Religious building (moderate size)
- Features: Dwarven-styled temple, community center
- Key NPC: Brother Thorin (dwarf priest)
- Function: Dwarven spiritual center

**4. The Foundry District**
- Location: Central/Eastern East Side
- Size: Large workshop district
- Features: Multiple blacksmith shops, metalworking
- Noise level: High (forge work)
- Function: Craft production center
- Key NPCs: Master Korran (blacksmith master), other smiths

**5. The Baker's Row**
- Location: Central East Side
- Size: Multiple bakery buildings clustered
- Features: Baking operations, bread production
- Key NPCs: Master Baker and cooperative
- Function: Bread and baked goods center

**6. The Brewhouse & Tavern**
- Location: Central East Side
- Size: Large brewery + attached tavern
- Features: Ale production, tavern space
- Key NPC: Grendel (brewery master)
- Function: Social and beverage center
- Tavern name: The Working Man's Rest

**7. Residential Areas**
- Location: Throughout East Side
- Size: Multiple small houses/multi-unit buildings
- Features: 50-70 family homes, modest design
- Function: Working-class housing

**8. Community Well**
- Location: Central gathering point
- Size: Well structure + plaza area
- Features: Water source, public gathering
- Function: Community infrastructure + social hub

**Craft Districts:**
- Group metalworkers together (Foundry)
- Group bakers together (Baker's Row)
- Place leatherworkers near market
- Place textile workers accessible
- Group tool makers centrally

## MAP ANNOTATION GUIDE

### If Creating Your Own Map:

**Grid Size:** 60-foot squares (typical)
- Downtown: 6-7 squares × 5 squares
- East Side: 13-14 squares × 10 squares
- Richman: 10 squares × 7 squares
- Lower Kilhel: 12 squares × 8 squares

**Building Sizes:**
- Shop: 1-2 squares wide
- Tavern/Inn: 2-3 squares
- Temple: 2-3 squares
- Warehouse: 2-4 squares
- Estate: 3-6+ squares with grounds

**Open Spaces:**
- Market plaza: 3-4 squares × 2-3 squares
- Streets: 1-2 squares wide (with buildings)
- Parks: 2-3 squares scattered

## SEASONAL MAP CHANGES

### Spring:
- Market expands (more vendor stalls)
- Construction activity visible
- Community gathering areas busier
- Travel routes clear/busier

### Summer:
- Maximum market activity
- Tourist season (more people)
- Evening outdoor activities
- Entertainment areas bustling

### Autumn:
- Agricultural focus visible
- Market emphasis changes
- Preparation activities
- Slightly less busy (harvest-focused)

### Winter:
- Market reduced
- Travel slower (weather)
- Indoor activities prominent
- Quieter streets
