## üè∞ CROSSROADS - OVERVIEW & CHARACTER

### Settlement Identity

**Crossroads Trade Outpost** is a fortified military-commercial hybrid settlement positioned at the junction of three major trade routes. Unlike Eldridge's merchant warmth, Crossroads feels *purposeful* and *defended*. It balances commerce with security, hospitality with caution. The atmosphere is professional, disciplined, and pragmatic‚Äîa place where trade happens under the watchful eye of militia command.

**Population feel:** ~60 permanent residents (20-25 militia, 15-20 merchants/merchants' families, remaining support staff/caravans). Smaller than Eldridge, but *more intense*. Everyone has a clear role. Strangers are assessed carefully‚Äîare you here for trade, trouble, or employment?

**Dominant aesthetic:** Fortified practicality. Stone foundation structures (more durable than timber), reinforced militia architecture, professional military bearing. Wooden palisade walls (well-maintained, regularly inspected). Stone-paved central courtyard (worn, but intentionally maintained). The effect is *secure but not unfriendly*‚Äîyou feel the military presence, but commerce is clearly valued.

## üåÑ ARRIVAL INTO CROSSROADS

### First Impressions (Approaching from Eldridge)

*When characters arrive at Crossroads from the west (from Eldridge on East Road), this is what they encounter:*

**The Approach (Half Mile Out):**
Long before reaching the settlement, you notice the *change*. The landscape flattens slightly, fields give way to more open terrain. The trade road straightens (more military surveying than natural topography). Then, rising above the grasslands, you see the palisade wall‚Äîwooden stakes, reinforced, clearly *defensible*. No casual approach here. Wooden watchtowers mark the corners (you can see militia scouts at the highest point, watching the road).

**Sensory Markers:**
- Smell shifts from open farmland to *military encampment*‚Äîwood, stone, cooking at scale, animal pens
- Sound: Before you hear people, you hear the *echo* of activity‚Äîmultiple voices, organized coordination
- Sight: The palisade dominates. Behind it, larger structures (warehouse, barracks) rise above the wall. Banners or militia marks are visible from distance
- The road narrows slightly as it approaches the gate (intentional choke point‚Äîdefensive)

**The Gate & Entry:**
Unlike Eldridge's welcoming open gate, Crossroads presents a *checkpoint*. The wooden palisade has a reinforced gate (substantial, can be closed). A small guardhouse sits beside it (always staffed). A militia guard (or two) stands watch.

**Initial Interaction:**
The guard looks up at your approach. Not hostile, but *assessing*. "Business at Crossroads?" or "Trade or militia business?" The tone is professional, not cold. You feel the weight of order and discipline.

**Passing Through:**
The gate is sturdy‚Äîyou can see repairs where it's been damaged (history of attacks). The ground inside is stone-paved (different from Eldridge's worn earth). The *temperature* feels cooler (stone absorbs heat differently). You're now *inside* a defensive structure‚Äîthe walls frame the settlement clearly.
