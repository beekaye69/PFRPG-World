## 1. EMPLOYMENT & DAILY WORK OPPORTUNITIES

### MERCHANT GUILD JOBS (Downtown)
**Frequency:** Daily availability  
**Intermediary:** Master Geldan (Merchant's Guild)  

**1. Market Guard Duty** (Ongoing)
- **Pay:** 5-8 sp per day
- **Work:** Protect market stalls from theft
- **Duration:** 4-8 hour shifts
- **Difficulty:** Minimal (mostly presence)
- **Contact:** Merchant Master Geldan

**2. Caravan Guard** (Weekly)
- **Pay:** 1-2 gp per day + food
- **Work:** Escort merchant caravan to nearby settlements
- **Duration:** 1-3 days round trip
- **Difficulty:** Low-Medium (occasional bandits)
- **Contacts:** Various merchant captains

**3. Goods Inspection** (As needed)
- **Pay:** 2-5 gp per job
- **Work:** Verify quality/quantity of incoming goods
- **Duration:** 2-4 hours
- **Difficulty:** Minimal (clerical, physical inspection)
- **Contact:** Merchant Master Geldan

**4. Dispute Mediation** (As needed)
- **Pay:** 5-10 gp per resolution
- **Work:** Resolve disputes between merchants
- **Duration:** Varies (1-4 hours)
- **Difficulty:** Low (social, negotiation)
- **Contact:** Merchant Master Geldan

## 3. MAJOR POLITICAL CONFLICTS

### CONFLICT 1: THE SUCCESSION QUESTION
**Parties:** Duke Aldric vs his son (Thomas Valorain, age 28)  
**Nature:** Generational dispute over leadership direction  
**Stakes:** Future of city governance, policy changes  

**Duke's Position:**
- Wants to gradually prepare son
- But maintains tight control of decisions
- Concerned about son's progressive ideas
- Wants stability above all

**Son's Position:**
- Wants modernization and change
- Feels Duke is too traditional/corrupt
- Impatient to lead
- Supported by younger nobles

**Current Status:**
- Tense but civil relationship
- Disagreements in council meetings
- Younger nobles taking sides
- No violent action yet

**Potential Escalations:**
- Son could attempt power grab
- Duke could disinherit son
- Noble factions could choose sides
- External pressure could force decision

**Player Opportunities:**
- Mediate between father/son
- Take side (with consequences)
- Exploit tensions for advantage
- Influence policy through relationships

---

### CONFLICT 2: MERCHANT VS CRAFT GUILDS
**Parties:** Merchant Master Geldan vs Craft Master Korran  
**Nature:** Economic power struggle (pricing, control)  
**Stakes:** City commerce organization, profit distribution  

**Merchant Position:**
- Want maximum prices (increase profit)
- Less regulation (more freedom)
- Merchant supremacy in city
- Control distribution channels

**Craft Position:**
- Want fair prices (maintain customer base)
- Quality standards (preserve reputation)
- Craft independence (not dominated)
- Fair profit margins

**Current Status:**
- Cold war (no open conflict)
- Competing for Duke's favor
- Undercutting where possible
- Blocking each other's initiatives

**Potential Escalations:**
- Open guild disputes
- Price wars affecting customers
- Duke forced to choose sides
- Criminal activity (sabotage)

**Player Opportunities:**
- Mediate dispute
- Take side (merchants or crafts)
- Exploit conflict for advantage
- Support one against other

---

### CONFLICT 3: RICHMAN ELITE RIVALRIES
**Parties:** Multiple noble families (Vellain primary)  
**Nature:** Status and power competition within nobility  
**Stakes:** Influence over Duke, social dominance, wealth  

**Key Figures:**
- Lord Vellain: Current power broker
- Rival families: Several competing for influence
- Younger nobles: Want fresh blood in leadership
- Family alliances: Shifting and complex

**Current Status:**
- Complex diplomatic maneuvering
- Strategic marriages possible
- Business rivalries
- Social competition

**Potential Escalations:**
- Assassination attempts
- Scandal revelations
- Business warfare
- Backing different successors

**Player Opportunities:**
- Play families against each other
- Solve internal conflicts
- Gather intelligence
- Take side in rivalries

---

### CONFLICT 4: CLASS TENSIONS
**Parties:** Upper class (Richman, wealthy) vs Lower class (East Side, Lower Kilhel)  
**Nature:** Economic disparity and opportunity gaps  
**Stakes:** Social stability, potential uprising  

**Upper Class Position:**
- Maintain status quo (benefits them)
- Emphasize law and order
- Justify inequality (merit/hard work)
- Resist change

**Lower Class Position:**
- Want better opportunities
- Feel exploited by system
- Seek fairness and equity
- Support reform

**Current Status:**
- Simmering resentment
- Occasional outbursts
- Guard keeping peace
- Status quo maintained

**Potential Escalations:**
- Labor strikes
- Protests and riots
- Criminal activity increases
- Revolutionary movements

**Player Opportunities:**
- Mediate class disputes
- Support reform efforts
- Suppress uprisings
- Exploit tensions
- Create lasting change

## 4. CAMPAIGN STORY ARCS

### ARC 1: THE SUCCESSION STRUGGLE (5-8 sessions)
**Scope:** Major political intrigue  
**Difficulty:** Medium-High (political complexity)  
**Themes:** Power, ambition, generational conflict  

**Act 1: The Tension Emerges**
- Players witness growing friction between Duke and son
- Invited to formal events, see political maneuvering
- Discover different factions supporting each side
- Made offers to take sides or broker peace

**Act 2: The Crisis Point**
- Son makes dramatic move (public challenge or betrayal)
- Duke must respond (forces confrontation)
- Noble factions mobilize
- Players must choose alliance or navigate carefully

**Act 3: Resolution (Multiple Paths)**
- Path A: Duke maintains control, son accepts role
- Path B: Son forces change, becomes new leader
- Path C: Compromise (shared power or governance change)
- Path D: Violence erupts (civil conflict)

**Consequences:**
- City policy changes based on winner
- Guard loyalties shift
- Merchant/craft balance changes
- Long-term implications for all campaigns

---

### ARC 2: THE MERCHANT CONSPIRACY (4-6 sessions)
**Scope:** Economic intrigue and crime mystery  
**Difficulty:** Medium (mystery/investigation)  
**Themes:** Corruption, commerce, hidden agendas  

**Act 1: The Discovery**
- Players notice suspicious merchant activity
- Black market goods appearing
- Tax revenue mysteriously low
- Guild disputes escalating

**Act 2: The Investigation**
- Interview key figures (merchants, guards, temple)
- Discover smuggling operation
- Uncover corruption (possibly official involvement)
- Piece together larger conspiracy

**Act 3: The Resolution**
- Confront merchant conspirators
- Decide how to handle (report, blackmail, join?)
- Choose guild loyalties
- Deal with fallout (price changes, power shifts)

**Consequences:**
- Market prices change based on outcome
- Guard power increases or decreases
- Merchant positions shift
- Long-term economic effects

---

### ARC 3: THE HIDDEN DANGER (6-10 sessions)
**Scope:** City-wide mystery with external threat  
**Difficulty:** Medium-High (discovery and confrontation)  
**Themes:** Mystery, danger, protecting city  

**Act 1: Strange Events**
- Peculiar occurrences in city
- Guard investigating mysterious crimes
- Temple reports concerning supernatural signs
- Merchant caravans report strange encounters outside city

**Act 2: The Truth Emerges**
- Players investigate (multiple clues available)
- Discover underlying threat (threat type varies by DM choice):
  - Criminal organization expanding into city
  - Supernatural creature/curse
  - Organized cult activity
  - External military threat
  - Magical anomaly

**Act 3: The Confrontation**
- Players must rally city defenders
- Prepare for confrontation
- Handle political complications
- Face the threat directly
- Deal with aftermath

**Consequences:**
- City unity increases
- Guard authority strengthens
- Survivors gained as allies
- Changes in city politics (threat bonds people or divides them)

## 5. QUICK ADVENTURE HOOKS (Grab-and-Go)

**1. The Missing Merchant** (1-2 sessions)
- Wealthy merchant missing 3 days
- Family worried, offers reward
- Investigation reveals kidnapping/accident/elopement
- Multiple solutions possible

**2. The Guild Dispute** (1 session)
- Two guild members fighting over contract
- Player asked to mediate
- Evidence contradicts both claims
- Must find truth and negotiate

**3. The Stolen Shipment** (2 sessions)
- Merchant caravan goods stolen
- Guard investigating (slowly)
- Players hired to recover goods
- Leads to local thieves/bandits

**4. The Temple Blessing** (1 session)
- Temple needs help with ceremony preparations
- Simple work becomes complicated
- Unexpected challenge emerges
- Reward: Blessing or temple favor

**5. The Competition** (2 sessions)
- Two craftspeople competing for contract
- Player asked to judge quality
- Personal drama complicates evaluation
- Must make fair decision

**6. The Noble Scandal** (2-3 sessions)
- Embarrassing situation for noble family
- Hired to retrieve compromising evidence
- Infiltration/negotiation required
- Political consequences

**7. The Guard Recruitment** (1 session)
- Guard short-staffed
- Temporary recruitment drive
- Players help with training/patrols
- Leads to larger issues

**8. The Market Disruption** (1 session)
- Market activities disrupted (problem varies)
- Guild asks for assistance
- Investigate and resolve issue
- Reward in grateful merchant support
